# Lake Primer

## Physical character
Flood-control reservoir (also called Gordon Pittock Reservoir) on the south branch of the Thames River, impounded by the Pittock Dam on the northeast edge of Woodstock. Built for flood control and summer low-flow augmentation, so water levels are managed through the season.

## Morphology signal
Mid-sized, long and narrow reservoir following the former river valley. Generally shallow, warm-water character with fluctuating managed levels; water clarity [NOT FOUND].

## Watershed & access
Part of the upper Thames River watershed, managed by the Upper Thames River Conservation Authority. Public access through Pittock Conservation Area, which supports shore fishing and boating with launch facilities.
