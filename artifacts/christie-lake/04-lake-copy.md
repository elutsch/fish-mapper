# Christie Lake

## Overview

Christie Lake is a flood-control reservoir with a dam bolted across Spencer Creek — 180 metres of it, holding back roughly 60 hectares of shallow, warm, weedy water in the Dundas hills west of Hamilton. This is Hamilton Conservation Authority ground: gated, day-use, and it fishes like the warm impoundment it is. Largemouth bass and northern pike run the show, with crappie and panfish filling in behind. Pike bury into the Spencer Creek inflow marsh at the west end, where the Christie Wildlife Area feeds the lake through the vegetation; largemouth work the causeway shallows and the docks off the Marina Pavilion, which is also your launch — electric motors only, no gas. One catch: the HCA closes the lake April 1 through the fourth Saturday of June, so your season really opens midsummer and runs to freeze-up. And there's no freeze-up fishing here — Christie never sets safe ice.

## Notable Facts
- The 180-metre Christie Dam was completed in 1971 to prevent major flooding in low-lying Dundas; the reservoir holds enough water to fill more than 900 Olympic-sized swimming pools. — https://conservationhamilton.ca/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Christie-Lake-Brochure-2026.pdf
- The reservoir sits inside the Niagara Escarpment World Biosphere Reserve and Ontario's Greenbelt, within a 353-hectare conservation area. — https://conservationhamilton.ca/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Christie-Lake-Brochure-2026.pdf
- HCA manages Christie as a sustainable native warm-water fishery and promotes a voluntary catch-and-release program to support it. — https://conservationhamilton.ca/activities/fishing/

## Largemouth Bass
**Tier:** Strong
**Best Season:** Summer through fall, from the late-June opener to freeze-up
**Structure:** Spencer Creek inflow marsh (Christie Wildlife Area), causeway shallows, Marina Pavilion docks

Largemouth own the salad here. Christie is shallow, warm, and weed-choked — everything a bucketmouth wants — and they hold in the marshy Spencer Creek inflow at the west end, along the causeway shallows, and under the Marina Pavilion docks. Bluegill and sunfish stock the pantry. Because the park stays closed until the fourth Saturday of June, you're on them post-spawn from day one: hit the pads and wood early and late through summer, then slide out to the deeper weed edges as fall cools the shallows down.

## Northern Pike
**Tier:** Strong
**Best Season:** Summer through fall (opens the fourth Saturday in June with the park)
**Structure:** Spencer Creek inflow / west-end marsh, Christie Dam basin

Nothing gets logged more often on Christie than pike — anglers put more of them in the book than largemouth. They haunt the vegetated Spencer Creek inflow at the west end, sliding out of the Christie Wildlife Area to ambush whatever runs the weed edge. The deepest water sits back at the 180-metre dam — the place to check when summer heat shoves fish off the shallow flats. The spring closure runs through late June, so you'll skip the classic early-pike window; plan on summer and fall, and write off winter, because Christie never sets safe ice.

Also swimming around down there: Black Crappie, tucked along the docks and causeway edges.
