# Morphology & history sources

## Town of Orangeville — Island Lake (OFFICIAL municipal)
URL: https://www.orangeville.ca/en/visit/island-lake.aspx  — accessed 2026-07-19
- "over 300 hectares of lake, wetland and forest"; "400 acres of lake"
- "development of the conservation area began in 1967 with the construction of two dams"
- "manmade reservoir was created through the flooding of a large cedar swamp, deciduous thicket, and small lake" — "to help augment the low flow conditions in the Credit River"
- Vicki Barron Lakeside Trail: eight-kilometre accessible loop; expansive boardwalks
- Species: "Largemouth Bass, Northern Pike, and Black Crappie"
- Ice fishing popular; open ice or hut. Part of Orangeville's landscape since 1970.

## CVC — More about Island Lake Conservation Area (OFFICIAL)
URL: https://cvc.ca/enjoy-the-outdoors/conservation-areas/island-lake-conservation-area/more-about-island-lake-conservation-area/ — accessed 2026-07-19

- Address: 673067 Hurontario St South, Orangeville

## Hiking the GTA — Island Lake (blog) 
URL: https://hikingthegta.com/2016/02/24/island-lake/ — accessed 2026-07-19
- "In 1967 it was decided to create a reservoir for Orangeville and the Island farm was flooded by the construction of two dams." Dams control outflow into the Credit River.
- Original property: a kettle lake, a cedar swamp, and a forest of mature trees.
- Depth: "Much of the lake is about 6 feet deep but there is one area marked at 22 feet." The 22-ft area corresponds to the original kettle lake.
- "These are the headwaters of the Credit River and will eventually flow into Lake Ontario."

## Adventure Fishing Maps — Island Lake (commercial map product)
URL: https://adventurefishing.ca/shop/fishing-map/southwestern-and-southern-ontario-fishing-maps/island-lake-southwestern-southern-ontario/ — accessed 2026-07-19
- Max depth: "the deepest area of the lake is located in the eastern arm where the depth reaches 20'+"
- Structure: "lots of fallen trees, submerged logs and stumps in the east end providing excellent cover for all the fish species. Extensive weed growth in the shallows do the same thing."
- Created 1967; 182 hectares; gas motors prohibited; FMZ 16.
- Major species: Largemouth Bass, Northern Pike, Yellow Perch, Black Crappie, Pumpkinseed, Catfish.
