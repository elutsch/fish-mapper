# Lake Primer

## Physical character
A flowing reach of the upper Saugeen River where it passes through the town of Durham, dropping over McGowan Falls beside a small flood-control dam in the Durham Conservation Area. This is a river reach, not a basin — runs, pools, and riffles through a valley, with a shallow recreational impoundment held above the dam.

## Morphology signal
Small upper-river reach: relatively narrow, cool, flowing water with gravel and rock substrate, deeper holding pools below the falls, and a warmer, slower impoundment behind the dam. Not a lake basin — no meaningful stratified depth or open-water clarity to describe as a still-water body.

## Watershed & access
Part of the Saugeen River watershed (draining to Lake Huron — NOT the Grand River system), managed as the Durham Conservation Area by the Saugeen Valley Conservation Authority. Access is through the conservation area in Durham, with a day-use parking fee and carry-in/paddle-friendly river frontage.

## Managing authorities
- Saugeen Valley Conservation Authority (SVCA)
- Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources (FMZ 16)

## Fishery reputation signal
The Durham reach is coldwater trout water — brook trout above the dam, with brown and rainbow trout in the river below — and that trout/steelhead fishery is its headline. Among warmwater/coolwater species, smallmouth bass are only marginally associated with this upper reach (documented smallmouth, pike, and musky fishing is downstream near Walkerton, not Durham).
