## Notable facts (defensible claims)

## Gotchas & disambiguation
- **Area 8 / Kelso Quarry conflation (major):** Area 8 is a separate former-quarry lake beside Kelso; its 2022–2025 stocking (brook trout, rainbow trout, yellow perch, smallmouth) is widely mis-attributed to Kelso Reservoir in search results. Fishing at Area 8 is currently prohibited except one-day derby events — https://www.conservationhalton.ca/parks/area-8/ ; https://www.conservationhalton.ca/activities/fishing/
- **Trout claims for the reservoir are stale/ambiguous:** the current Conservation Halton species list for Kelso Lake omits trout; older third-party lists (Tourism Burlington, Active Parents) include rainbow and brown trout, and trout catches exist in community logs. Treat reservoir trout as low-confidence; the solid trout story is the creek below the dam (steelhead) and upstream (resident browns) — sources as cited above.
- **Park bait rule stricter than provincial law:** worms only, purchased from a bait supplier; no live minnows, crayfish, or frogs; lead tackle discouraged — https://www.conservationhalton.ca/parks/kelso/
- **No-fishing zones:** beach, boardwalk, Pump House + parking lot, Kelso Cove Waterpark, boat-rentals beach, and the dam — https://www.conservationhalton.ca/parks/kelso/
- **Paid entry & booking:** day-use fee applies; reservations recommended via parkvisit.ca — https://www.conservationhalton.ca/parks/kelso/
- **Name confusion:** distinct from Kelso Lake (Idaho), Kelso (Washington), and the adjacent Area 8/Kelso Quarry lake; also distinct from Crawford Lake (no fishing) and Mill Pond in Milton.
- **FMZ:** taxonomy FMZ 16 confirmed against official sources — no discrepancy to flag.
