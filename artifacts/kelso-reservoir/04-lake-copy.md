# Kelso Reservoir

## Overview

Kelso Reservoir is a small warm-water impoundment — about 30 hectares — bolted onto Sixteen Mile Creek where it drops off the Niagara Escarpment at Milton. Conservation Halton threw the dam up in 1964 to keep the creek from flooding downtown Milton every spring thaw, and the flood-control basin it left behind is the water you fish today. Limestone cliffs wall one shore; a boardwalk and a swimming beach line another; the shallow, weedy "Pirate's Cove" juts off the middle. This is a casual family fishery — largemouth bass and yellow perch carry it, with rock bass, sunfish, and the odd crappie filling in. Non-motorized boats only, electric trolling motors excepted, off the small ramp inside Kelso Conservation Area. Shore fishing is open too — just not off the beach, the boardwalk, or the dam.

## Notable Facts
- Kelso Dam and Reservoir opened in 1964 by the conservation authority to control flooding on Sixteen Mile Creek. — https://www.conservationhalton.ca/about-us/history/
- The Sixteen-Mile Creek Authority bought the land in 1961 for $40,000 and built a $325,000 water-control dam; Milton's main street previously flooded every spring thaw. — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kelso_Conservation_Area
- The reservoir doubles as the cold-water source for snow-making at Glen Eden Ski & Snowboard Centre. — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kelso_Conservation_Area
- Kelso is one of the few Conservation Halton reservoirs where boat fishing (non-motorized or electric) is permitted, alongside Mountsberg. — https://www.conservationhalton.ca/activities/fishing/

## Largemouth Bass
**Tier:** Strong
**Best Season:** Late-June opener through fall
**Structure:** shallow weedy flats at the beach/inflow (northwest) end; "Pirate's Cove" shallow jut; boardwalk shoreline margins

Largemouth own this reservoir — they're the most-logged catch here by a wide margin, ahead of perch and pumpkinseed. It's exactly the water they want: warm, shallow, weedy. Work the flats at the beach-and-inflow end in the northwest, the cover in Pirate's Cove, and the shaded margins along the boardwalk shore. You can't cast off the boardwalk itself, but the cover beside it still holds fish. From the late-June opener through fall, stick to the shallow green stuff and the cover edges — this is a shallow-water game start to finish.

## Yellow Perch
**Tier:** Strong
**Best Season:** Open all year; most reliable shore bite May through October
**Structure:** boardwalk and shoreline margins (shore-accessible); mid-lake basin

Perch are the sure thing at Kelso. No fish here gets confirmed more often by the anglers who log their catches, and most of it happens within reach of the bank. Fan the shoreline margins where the boardwalk shore drops into fishable water, or push out to the deeper mid-lake basin from a canoe or kayak. The season never closes on paper, but the dependable shore bite lines up with the park's May-through-October run. Small hooks, worked slow along the edges — this is the fishery that puts a kid's first fish on the dock.

Also swimming around down there: smallmouth bass hugging the dam-end riprap and the escarpment rubble, black crappie in ones and twos out over the basin.
