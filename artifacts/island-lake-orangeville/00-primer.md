# Lake Primer

## Physical character
Man-made reservoir at the headwaters of the Credit River on the edge of Orangeville, created by two dams (built late 1960s) that flooded a cedar swamp and small natural lake to augment Credit River low flows. Sits inside Island Lake Conservation Area.

## Morphology signal
Mid-sized reservoir, shallow and weedy character — a flooded wetland/swamp basin rather than a deep impoundment, with islands, wetland edges, and boardwalk-crossed shallows. Warm-water habitat.

## Watershed & access
Headwaters of the Credit River watershed, managed by Credit Valley Conservation as a conservation-area park. Public access through the conservation area with paddle-craft launching and rentals; ice fishing access in winter. Motor restrictions to be confirmed by Stage 1a.

## Managing authorities
- Credit Valley Conservation (CVC)
- Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources (FMZ 16)
- Town of Orangeville (adjacent municipal partner)

## Fishery reputation signal
Reservoir known for northern pike, largemouth bass, and panfish — black crappie, yellow perch, pumpkinseed, bluegill — with active ice fishing and twice-yearly derbies.
