# Lake Primer

## Physical character
Natural kettle lake in the Township of Puslinch, east of Cambridge — reported as the largest kettle lake in the region. Spring- and runoff-fed with no permanent inflow streams; outflows into the Grand River drainage only during high water. Waterbody type confirmed: natural-lake (taxonomy's unconfirmed flag resolved). FMZ 16 confirmed against Ontario's FMZ 16 (Southwestern Ontario) coverage — no taxonomy discrepancy.

## Morphology signal
Mid-sized lake, predominantly shallow and weedy throughout, suiting warm-water species; not a cold-water or deep-basin fishery.

## Watershed & access
Part of the Grand River drainage basin (intermittent outflow). Shoreline is largely private cottage/residential; the former public beach and boat launch closed to the public in 2020, and the township has since discussed restoring public access — practical access is currently limited and should be documented carefully by Stage 1a.

## Managing authorities
- Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources (FMZ 16)
- Grand River Conservation Authority
- Township of Puslinch
- Puslinch Lake Conservation Association (local stewardship group)

## Fishery reputation signal
Known mainly for abundant largemouth bass in the shallows, with black crappie present (historically stocked) and walleye stocked on and off over the decades with limited natural reproduction. Historic muskellunge and smallmouth bass stockings did not establish populations.
