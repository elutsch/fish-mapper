## In-lake structure (by species)

### Smallmouth Bass
- The Paris-to-Brantford "Exceptional Waters" reach — the wadeable run-pool-riffle river itself, rock/gravel/rubble bottom, current seams and pockets behind rock — is the core smallmouth water; the reach is managed as a barbless, artificial-lure, catch-and-release zone specifically for its bass fishery — https://www.grandriver.ca/our-watershed/fisheries-management/exceptional-waters
- Current seams and pool tail-outs where faster water meets slower holding water over gravel/rubble — classic river-smallmouth ambush lies in a reach averaging ~1 m deep — https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/fishing/fishing-rivers-and-streams/
- Deep water above Wilkes Dam (downstream end, Brantford) — deep holes and structure from rubble and old dam remnants hold smallmouth (also walleye and pike) — https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/fishing/fishing-rivers-and-streams/ (Wilkes Dam / Brant Conservation Area frontage)
- Whitemans Creek confluence (between Paris and Brantford) — cold-water inflow creating a temperature refuge and feeding lane — https://www.grandriver.ca/our-watershed/fisheries-management/exceptional-waters

### Northern Pike
- Slower runs, deeper pools, and the deep water above Wilkes Dam — the reach's holding water where a river pike ambushes from current breaks and slack margins — https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/fishing/fishing-rivers-and-streams/
- Slack backwaters and the slower millpond water above Penman's Dam in Paris — soft-current holding water suited to pike — https://1000towns.ca/penmans-dam-on-the-grand-river/

### Walleye
- Deep water and holes above Wilkes Dam (Brantford) — rubble and old-dam structure that concentrates walleye in the reach — https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/fishing/fishing-rivers-and-streams/
- Current areas and cold-water inflow near Whitemans Creek — the cooler, oxygenated flowing water the GRCA credits for supporting walleye in this reach — https://www.grandriver.ca/our-watershed/fisheries-management/exceptional-waters

### Black Crappie
- Slower pools and slack backwaters / the millpond above Penman's Dam — softer current where crappie hold in a flowing reach — https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/fishing/fishing-rivers-and-streams/ (crappie listed among reach species; slack-water association is the general habitat pattern)

## Seasonal patterns
- Best season(s) by species: Smallmouth — summer through early fall in the wadeable runs and pools (bass are catch-and-release here); walleye and pike — spring and fall in deeper holes and current areas.
- Spawn windows: Smallmouth spawn late spring/early summer on gravel; the base bass season opens the fourth Saturday in June (post-spawn); walleye and pike spawn in early spring, before the base May opener.
- Turnover / thermocline notes: Not applicable — flowing river, no stratified basin. Cold-water inflows (Whitemans Creek, springs) create localized temperature refuges in summer.
- Ice fishing: Not applicable / not documented for this flowing in-town reach; the March 1–April sanctuary closes the Paris-to-Brantford water in early spring.

## Notable facts (defensible claims)
- The Grand River between Paris and Brantford is a designated "Exceptional Waters" reach — the fishing season was extended and special barbless-hook, artificial-lure, catch-and-release regulations were put in place to manage the fishery — https://www.grandriver.ca/our-watershed/fisheries-management/exceptional-waters
- Cold water entering from Whitemans Creek and springs makes the reach good habitat for smallmouth bass, walleye, northern pike and rainbow trout, and supports species at risk including black redhorse and river redhorse — https://www.grandriver.ca/our-watershed/fisheries-management/exceptional-waters
- The Grand River (Paris) special regulation makes brown trout, northern pike, rainbow trout, smallmouth bass and walleye strictly catch-and-release (S-0 / C-0) with artificial lures and a single barbless hook — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16
- The Nith River joins the Grand at Paris — the "Forks of the Grand" — adding flow to the reach — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_River_(Ontario)
- Penman's Dam in Paris was built in 1918 by the Penman clothing company for mill waterpower and is now a decorative feature (Willow Street) — https://1000towns.ca/penmans-dam-on-the-grand-river/
- Across angler catch logs for the Grand River, smallmouth bass are the most-logged species, followed by northern pike and walleye (source: angler report — user-contributed) — https://www.anglersatlas.com/place/723165/grand-river

## Gotchas & disambiguation
- This is the Grand River reach at Paris, Ontario (FMZ 16), not a lake. Do not confuse with same-named rivers elsewhere.
- The special catch-and-release regulation is easy to violate: smallmouth, walleye, and pike must be released, only artificial lures are allowed, and only a single barbless hook — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16
- Two seasonal sanctuary closures apply (Oct 1–Nov 30 near Penman Dam; March 1 to before the fourth-Saturday-in-April bass opener, Paris to Brantford) — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16
- No trailer boat launch on this reach — wade, canoe, or drift boat only.
- Angler's Atlas catch frequencies are user-contributed (lower-trust); used only as a relative popularity signal, never for regulations.
