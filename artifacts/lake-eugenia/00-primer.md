# Lake Primer

## Physical character
Artificial hydro-electric reservoir created by damming the Beaver River near Flesherton, at the top of the Beaver Valley on the Niagara Escarpment in Grey Highlands. Flooded former farmland, leaving submerged stumps and logs across the basin.

## Morphology signal
Mid-sized reservoir, predominantly shallow and weedy — extensive cabbage, reed, and lily pad beds with abundant woody cover — with a limited deeper zone. Warm-water character overall, ringed by cottages.

## Watershed & access
Headwaters reservoir of the Beaver River system (drains toward Georgian Bay at Thornbury). Public municipal boat launch and beach access maintained by the Municipality of Grey Highlands.

## Managing authorities
- Ontario Power Generation (hydro reservoir / dam operator)
- Municipality of Grey Highlands (public access)

## Fishery reputation signal
Known as a largemouth and smallmouth bass fishery in heavy weed and timber cover, with yellow perch and sunfish, plus stocked rainbow trout run as a put-and-take fishery.
