### Smallmouth Bass
- Reservoir main body / deep-water structure toward the dam arm — angler reports describe Guelph Lake as "smallmouth heaven" in summer with trophy fish (>50 cm) relating to deep-water structure; smallmouth are the single most-logged species on the lake (Fishbrain: 173 anglers) — (source: angler report — user-contributed) https://trekandangle.com/fly-fishing-at-guelph-lake-for-smallmouth-bass/ ; https://fishbrain.com/fishing-waters/glRE4oS7/guelph-lake
- Conservation-area (west) basin — GRCA notes both largemouth and smallmouth in the conservation-area portion west of Wellington Rd 124; shoreline fan-casting productive along rubble/hard shoreline — (source: angler report + GRCA) https://hooklineandsinker.ca/guelph-lake-conservation-area/

### Largemouth Bass
- Weedy bays and the eastern arm — largemouth dominate "to the east" of the lake and in weedy back-bay water; anglers report fishing "a very weedy bay"; spinnerbaits and crankbaits produce mid-water bass and pike over the weeds — (source: angler report — user-contributed) https://hooklineandsinker.ca/guelph-lake-conservation-area/
- Shallow vegetated river-arm flats (upper/east end off Wellington Rd 124/29) — warm, shallow, weed-choked water on the east side of the lake matches largemouth habitat — (source: angler report — user-contributed) https://hooklineandsinker.ca/guelph-lake-conservation-area/

### Northern Pike
- Weed beds / weed edges lake-wide — pike taken over mid-water weed structure on spinnerbaits and crankbaits; pike are among the most-confirmed species (Anglers Atlas, Fishbrain 146 anglers) — (source: angler report + Fishbrain) https://hooklineandsinker.ca/guelph-lake-conservation-area/ ; https://fishbrain.com/fishing-waters/glRE4oS7/guelph-lake
