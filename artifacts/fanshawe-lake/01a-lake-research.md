### Smallmouth Bass
- Rock/gravel and rubble bottom areas ("gravel bottoms or near large rocks, logs or stumps") — the lake's documented sand/clay/**rock** substrate provides smallmouth habitat; deeper, clearer water toward the Fanshawe Dam (southwest) fits their preference for hard structure — https://www.fanshaweconservationarea.ca/_files/ugd/95bf18_da90df0efeda49ebbc3b06458b400238.pdf ; https://adventurefishing.ca/shop/fishing-map/southwestern-and-southern-ontario-fishing-maps/fanshawe-lake-southwestern-southern-ontario/
- Most-reported species on Fishbrain (62 members), indicating a well-established smallmouth population — https://fishbrain.com/fishing-waters/JwdEDfuO/fanshawe-lake (source: angler report — user-contributed)

### Largemouth Bass
- Shallow, weedy water near dense vegetation for cover (brochure habitat note); the reservoir's shallow upper (north) end and vegetated backwaters/bays off the drowned river valley suit largemouth — https://www.fanshaweconservationarea.ca/_files/ugd/95bf18_da90df0efeda49ebbc3b06458b400238.pdf

### Northern Pike
- Weedy shorelines and cool weedy bays — brochure lists pike habitat as "cool lakes, large ponds, rivers, near weedy shoreline"; vegetated margins and the North Thames inflow area provide ambush and spawning cover — https://www.fanshaweconservationarea.ca/_files/ugd/95bf18_da90df0efeda49ebbc3b06458b400238.pdf

### Walleye
- North Thames River inflow (north end) and the old river channel through the reservoir — walleye "adapt to a variety of water conditions" and are "very light sensitive," fitting the turbid/stained river-fed water and current areas at the inflow; deeper basin toward the dam holds fish in daylight — https://www.fanshaweconservationarea.ca/_files/ugd/95bf18_da90df0efeda49ebbc3b06458b400238.pdf ; https://adventurefishing.ca/shop/fishing-map/southwestern-and-southern-ontario-fishing-maps/fanshawe-lake-southwestern-southern-ontario/

## Gotchas & disambiguation
- Disambiguation confirmed: this is the UTRCA reservoir on the North Thames River at London (43.0583, -81.1786), not any other Ontario waterbody. Anglers Atlas spells it "Fanshaw Lake."
- Motor restriction is strict: only electric or sub-10-hp combustion motors, for fishing only — plan tackle/boat accordingly — https://www.fanshaweconservationarea.ca/fishingandboating
- On-water zoning: rowing racing course (National High Performance Rowing Centre) and Fanshawe Yacht Club sailing course occupy parts of the lake; buoyed areas prohibit all vessels — https://www.fanshaweconservationarea.ca/_files/ugd/95bf18_da90df0efeda49ebbc3b06458b400238.pdf
- Water quality: 2009 blue-green algae bloom led to a swimming ban; eutrophic, nutrient-rich water — algae advisories possible in summer — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fanshawe_Lake
- Do not dump crayfish/baitfish into the lake unless taken from it (UTRCA rule) — invasive/bait caution — https://www.fanshaweconservationarea.ca/_files/ugd/95bf18_da90df0efeda49ebbc3b06458b400238.pdf
