# Lake Primer

## Physical character
Man-made reservoir at the headwaters of the Credit River on the edge of Orangeville, created by two dams that flooded a cedar swamp, a thicket, and a small kettle lake to augment Credit River low flows. Sits inside Island Lake Conservation Area.

## Morphology signal
Shallow, weedy, warm-water reservoir — a flooded wetland basin rather than a deep clear impoundment, with islands, submerged and standing timber, extensive weed growth, and one deeper hole in the eastern arm over the old kettle lake. Water character likely stained.

## Watershed & access
Headwaters of the Credit River, managed by Credit Valley Conservation as a conservation-area park. Public access through the conservation area — trailer and hand launch, paddlecraft rentals, gas motors prohibited, ice fishing in winter.

## Managing authorities
- Credit Valley Conservation (CVC)
- Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources (FMZ 16)
- Town of Orangeville (municipal partner)

## Fishery reputation signal
Reservoir known for northern pike and largemouth bass, with black crappie, yellow perch, and other panfish, and an active winter ice fishery with an annual derby.
