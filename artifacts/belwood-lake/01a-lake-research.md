# Lake Research: Belwood Lake

**FMZ verification:** Taxonomy assigns FMZ 16 and this is CONFIRMED. Belwood Lake sits on the upper Grand River north of Fergus (Wellington County). The Ontario MNR FMZ 16 page describes the zone as "all of southwestern Ontario … predominantly large river systems," and multiple independent waterbody databases place Belwood explicitly in Zone 16 (Anglers Atlas; Adventure Fishing Maps). No discrepancy to flag.

- Water clarity / colour — stained/turbid ("notably murky"); algal growth promoted by nutrient enrichment — https://www.thefishinguide.com/belwood-lake.php ; https://atrium.lib.uoguelph.ca/server/api/core/bitstreams/4477bcf8-110b-4083-877c-31092c557f5c/content
- Trophic status — mesotrophic (nutrient-enriched, buildup within reservoir; hard/alkaline limestone water, pH ~8) — https://atrium.lib.uoguelph.ca/server/api/core/bitstreams/4477bcf8-110b-4083-877c-31092c557f5c/content
- Thermal behaviour — stratifies "to a mild degree" during summer despite bottom draw-off — https://atrium.lib.uoguelph.ca/server/api/core/bitstreams/4477bcf8-110b-4083-877c-31092c557f5c/content
- Reservoir dynamics — flood-control impoundment behind Shand Dam (earthfill, 78 ft high, 2,100 ft long, completed 1942; storage 51,788 acre-ft at max level; drainage area 308.5 sq mi). Higher water in spring/summer, gradual fall drawdown (~150 acre-ft/day early summer, ~300 acre-ft/day late summer/fall); maintenance drawdowns can reach ~1 ft (30 cm)/day toward ~415 m elevation. Elevation ~422 m ASL. — https://atrium.lib.uoguelph.ca/server/api/core/bitstreams/4477bcf8-110b-4083-877c-31092c557f5c/content ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Belwood

## Seasonal patterns
- Best seasons by species (evergreen): Smallmouth bass strongest spring through summer, notably evening bites; pike targeted open-water and through the ice; walleye reported around the May opener and in winter; perch a mainstay ice-fishing target. — https://www.thefishinguide.com/belwood-lake.php ; https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/fishing/fishing-grand-river-conservation-areas/
- Spawn windows: Common carp spawn in the shallows in May (observed mid-May) — https://argosgirloutdoors.com/2013/05/20/walleye-in-the-sun-at-belwood-lake/ ; walleye active near the 2nd-Saturday-of-May opener (regional pattern, post-spawn) — https://argosgirloutdoors.com/2013/05/20/walleye-in-the-sun-at-belwood-lake/
- Turnover / thermocline: Mildly stratifies in summer despite bottom draw-off; expect a modest thermocline and warmwater surface layer. Fall drawdown lowers levels and concentrates fish toward the deeper river channel/basin. — https://atrium.lib.uoguelph.ca/server/api/core/bitstreams/4477bcf8-110b-4083-877c-31092c557f5c/content
- Ice fishing: Popular for perch, walleye and pike when ice/weather permit; ice measured 10–12 inches in a documented February outing; vehicles have been driven onto the ice; temporary ice huts allowed but must be removed daily. Check GRCA Activity Status before going. Low winter reservoir levels expose shoreline and create steep drops into the main basin that hold fish. — https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/fishing/fishing-grand-river-conservation-areas/ ; https://argosgirloutdoors.com/2012/02/13/belwood-lake-ice-fishing-february-12-2012/

## Gotchas & disambiguation
- Name confusion: "Lake Belwood" vs "Belwood Lake" used interchangeably; also a separate "Belwood Lake (Quarry)" swimming site exists — this profile is the GRCA reservoir behind Shand Dam on the upper Grand, north of Fergus. — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Belwood
- Tailwater vs reservoir: brown trout, artificial-lure/barbless rules, and the fish sanctuary are Grand RIVER (below/around the dam) regulations — do NOT apply them to the reservoir. Reservoir uses FMZ 16 zone defaults. — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16
- Reservoir drawdown hazard: water levels vary substantially (fall drawdown, maintenance drawdowns to ~415 m); GRCA posts hazard signage at the launch for exposed hazards/mudflats. Navigation hazards increase at low water. — https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/conservation-areas/belwood-lake/ ; https://atrium.lib.uoguelph.ca/server/api/core/bitstreams/4477bcf8-110b-4083-877c-31092c557f5c/content
- Bait Management Zone: transport restrictions on live/dead baitfish and leeches (Southern BMZ) — verify before using bait. — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16
- Muskellunge and largemouth bass appear only in user-logged catch data (Fishbrain), not official MNR survey/Anglers-Atlas-confirmed inventories — treat as unconfirmed/incidental. — https://fishbrain.com/fishing-waters/91L2cSz5/lake-belwood
