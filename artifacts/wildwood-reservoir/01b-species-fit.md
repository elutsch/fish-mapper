# Species Fit: Wildwood Reservoir

**FMZ verification:** Taxonomy says FMZ 16 — CONFIRMED, no discrepancy. The UTRCA's own fishing page states the Thames River watershed falls within Fisheries Management Zone 16, and the official ontario.ca FMZ 16 summary lists the Thames system among its zone-16 waterbodies, placing the upper Thames / Trout Creek impoundment in FMZ 16 (https://thamesriver.on.ca/parks-recreation-natural-areas/fishing/; https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16).

## Summary
- **Destination species:** none
- **Strong species:** Northern Pike, Yellow Perch
- **Headline structure surfaced:** the deep dam-wall basin at the west end (retains the most water through the winter drawdown; holds wintering perch schools in 12–15 ft and pike in the deepest holes) and the spring-refill drawdown-zone shallows of the S-shaped upper reaches (flooded banks that re-inundate during pike spawn)

This is a solid local warm-water flood-control reservoir, not a destination fishery. No MNR survey/CPUE, tournament history, documented trophy, or stocking program was found for any species here (the fishery is self-sustaining — no Fish ON-Line stocking records), so no species qualifies for Destination. Named, documented structure exists only for pike and perch, both anchored on the dam basin; smallmouth and largemouth are documented as present but with no named structure, capping them at Present.
