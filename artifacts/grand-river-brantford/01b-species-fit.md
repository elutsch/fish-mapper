# Species Fit: Grand River at Brantford

Framing: this is a flowing-river reach (the Paris-to-Brantford stretch of the Grand
River, at Brantford), not a lake basin. Species are scored on river-appropriate
structure — riffles, runs, pools, current seams, rock/gravel, slack water below
Wilkes Dam. Channel catfish, a documented and locally renowned fishery below the
Brantford dam, is OUT OF SCOPE (not in the six-species taxonomy) and is not scored.

## Summary
- **Destination species:** Smallmouth Bass
- **Strong species:** Northern Pike, Walleye
- **Headline structure surfaced:** riffle-run-pool sequences over rock and gravel through the Paris-to-Brantford "Exceptional Waters" reach; slack water and pools below Wilkes Dam; deeper runs holding walleye and pike off the current.

## Scoring detail

### Smallmouth Bass
- **Tier:** Destination
- **Structure surfaced:** riffle-run-pool water over rock and gravel through the Paris-to-Brantford reach; slack water and pools below Wilkes Dam; the Exceptional Waters reach generally.
- **Evidence signal:** the Paris-to-Brantford stretch is a provincially designated "Exceptional Waters Reach," designated in part for the high quality of its fishing and managed under special catch-and-release regulations (artificial lures, single barbless hook, S-0/C-0 for smallmouth); most-logged species on the Grand across angler-observation data (24 logs). A managed special-regulation designation tied to fishing quality is a citable quality signal.
- **Reasoning:** Flowing rock-and-gravel river reach with riffle/run/pool structure and cold tributary input — prime smallmouth water — carrying a named provincial designation and a catch-and-release fishery built specifically to protect its quality. This is a species anglers travel to fish.
- **Sources:** https://www.grandriver.ca/our-watershed/fisheries-management/exceptional-waters ; https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/fishing/fishing-rivers-and-streams/ ; https://www.anglersatlas.com/place/723165/grand-river

### Northern Pike
- **Tier:** Strong
- **Structure surfaced:** slack water, pools, and weedy margins below Wilkes Dam; slower varied-habitat water toward Brantford-to-Caledonia.
- **Evidence signal:** a documented shore/canoe target at Brant Conservation Area below Wilkes Dam; second most-logged species on the Grand (22 logs).
- **Reasoning:** Pike hold in the slack water off the current and in weedy margins — named holding water below Wilkes Dam and documented presence give a clear, specific reason to target them here, above generic presence.
- **Sources:** https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/conservation-areas/brant/ ; https://www.anglersatlas.com/place/723165/grand-river

### Walleye
- **Tier:** Strong
- **Structure surfaced:** deeper runs and pools, and the slack water below Wilkes Dam; the reach as part of the noted lower-Grand walleye fishery.
- **Evidence signal:** documented "pickerel" target at Brant Conservation Area; a noted lower-Grand walleye fishery that opens in May and fishes through the November bass close; 13 angler logs.
- **Reasoning:** Low-light river feeder holding the deeper runs and the break below the dam — named holding water and a documented walleye fishery on this stretch make a specific case beyond mere presence.
- **Sources:** https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/conservation-areas/brant/ ; https://driftoutfitters.com/blogs/river-resources/grand-river-lower-section
