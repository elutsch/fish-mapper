# Species Fit: Grand River at Paris

## Summary
- **Destination species:** Smallmouth Bass
- **Strong species:** Northern Pike, Walleye
- **Headline structure surfaced:** the Paris-to-Brantford "Exceptional Waters" run-pool-riffle reach over rock/gravel/rubble, current seams and pool tail-outs, deep water above Wilkes Dam, and the cold-water Whitemans Creek confluence.

## Scoring detail

### Smallmouth Bass
- **Tier:** Destination
- **Structure surfaced:** the wadeable Paris-to-Brantford reach itself (rock/gravel/rubble bottom, current seams, pool tail-outs, pockets behind rock); deep holes and rubble above Wilkes Dam; the Whitemans Creek cold-water confluence.
- **Evidence signal:** The Grand between Paris and Brantford is a provincially designated "Exceptional Waters" reach, deliberately managed with an extended season and barbless-hook / artificial-lure / catch-and-release-only regulations to protect a high-quality smallmouth fishery (GRCA). Smallmouth are also the most-logged Grand River species in angler catch data (Angler's Atlas — user-contributed, corroborating signal only).
- **Reasoning:** People travel to fish this reach specifically for river smallmouth; the "Exceptional Waters" designation plus a species-driven special regulation is a genuine, citable management/quality signal, and the reach's structure is named and documented. Meets Destination on both required legs.
- **Sources:** https://www.grandriver.ca/our-watershed/fisheries-management/exceptional-waters ; https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/fishing/fishing-rivers-and-streams/
