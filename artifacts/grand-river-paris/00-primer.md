# Lake Primer

## Physical character
A flowing reach of the Grand River through the town of Paris, Ontario — a wadeable warm-water river, not a basin. The Nith River joins the Grand here (the "Forks of the Grand"), and the historic Penman's Dam sits in town.

## Morphology signal
Shallow, wadeable river — mostly hip-to-chest depth over a mix of rock, gravel, and rubble, with runs, pools, and riffles rather than open-lake water. No lake-style surface area or max-depth character applies. Water is generally clear-to-lightly-stained flowing current; deeper holes form above dams.

## Watershed & access
Part of the Grand River watershed (a Canadian Heritage River), managed for fisheries by the Grand River Conservation Authority with Ontario MNR setting FMZ 16 regulations. Access is via municipal parks in Paris, riverside trails, and carry-in points for wading, canoe, or drift boat — no trailer ramp on this reach.

## Managing authorities
- Grand River Conservation Authority
- Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources (FMZ 16)
- County of Brant / municipal parks (shore access)

## Fishery reputation signal
Known as one of Ontario's better smallmouth bass rivers — the Paris-to-Brantford stretch is a designated "Exceptional Waters" reach with special catch-and-release regulations. Walleye and northern pike are also present, with crappie, rock bass, sunfish, carp, and channel catfish in slower water.
