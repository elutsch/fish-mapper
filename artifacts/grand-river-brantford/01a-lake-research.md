### Northern Pike

### Waterbody exception — Grand River, Paris to Brantford (GOVERNS THIS REACH)
Boundary (verbatim): from a line across the Grand River in Paris at 100 metres downstream of the Highway 2 (Dundas Street West) bridge downstream to the pedestrian and service bridge in Brantford.
- Only artificial lures may be used — effective 2025-12-08 — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16
- Only 1 barbless hook may be used — effective 2025-12-08 — same source
- Fish sanctuary — no fishing from March 1 to Friday before fourth Saturday in April — effective 2025-12-08 — same source
- Brown trout, northern pike, rainbow trout, smallmouth bass and walleye — S-0 and C-0 (catch-and-release only) — effective 2025-12-08 — same source
- Species NOT named in the S-0/C-0 line (largemouth bass, black crappie, yellow perch) fall under FMZ 16 zone-wide limits above; the artificial-lure / single-barbless-hook / sanctuary rules apply to the whole reach.

## Notable facts (defensible claims)
- The Grand River was designated a Canadian Heritage River in 1994; it is the longest river entirely within southwestern Ontario and supports more than 82 fish species. — https://chrs.ca/en/rivers/grand-river
- The Paris-to-Brantford stretch is designated an "Exceptional Waters Reach," in part for the high quality of its fishing, and carries special provincial regulations (artificial lures, single barbless hook, catch-and-release for smallmouth bass, walleye, pike and trout). — https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/fishing/fishing-rivers-and-streams/ ; https://www.grandriver.ca/our-watershed/fisheries-management/exceptional-waters

- Cold water entering from Whitemans Creek and springs, plus banks lined by largely untouched Carolinian forest, help sustain the reach's fishery and host species at risk (black redhorse, river redhorse). — https://www.grandriver.ca/our-watershed/fisheries-management/exceptional-waters
