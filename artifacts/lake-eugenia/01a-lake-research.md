## Notable facts (defensible claims)

- One of Ontario's older hydro reservoirs: dam on the Beaver River built 1912; ~1,900 acres of farmland flooded by the Hydro Electric Power Commission after 1913 — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Eugenia
- Feeds OPG's Eugenia Falls Generating Station (in service 1915, ~6 MW, 3 units), which exploits the Niagara Escarpment drop at Eugenia Falls — OPG water portal / search-corroborated; station history also at https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Eugenia (1912 dam) [station specs snippet-sourced]
- Extreme shallow-bowl morphology: ~40 ft max depth against a ~4.6 ft mean depth — flooded farmland full of stumps and standing timber — https://adventurefishing.ca/shop/sso/eugenia-lake-southwestern-southern-ontario/
- Smallmouth and largemouth are the two most-caught species on user catch logs (59 and 42 of 288 logged Fishbrain catches) — https://fishbrain.com/fishing-waters/0EAXs1RS/eugenia-lake (user-contributed data)
- The only lake in Grey Highlands with municipal public access allowing motorized boats — https://www.greyhighlands.ca/our-community/parks-lakes-and-trails/lakes/ (paraphrase of municipal/tourism description)

## Gotchas & disambiguation

- Access bottleneck: single public access point (Canrobert St); everything else is private cottage shoreline; motorboat launch is fee/pass-based and seasonally gated (2026 season from May 8 — before this, trailer access may be unavailable) — https://www.greyhighlands.ca/our-community/parks-lakes-and-trails/lakes/
- Name confusion: the FMZ 16 sanctuary on "Beaver River — Thorah Township (Beaverton)" is a DIFFERENT Beaver River (Lake Simcoe watershed), not the Grey County Beaver River below Lake Eugenia. Do not apply it to this lake — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16
- Below-dam waters are a different regulatory regime: the Grey County Beaver River (downstream) carries its own exceptions and is managed as trout/steelhead water; lake regs stop at the dam — same source
- Rainbow trout "put-and-take" reputation is not backed by current official stocking records — do not promote a trout fishery without Stage 2 verification (see Stocking history flag)
- Submerged stumps and logs throughout the basin are a navigation hazard, especially with winter/spring water-level changes on this OPG-managed reservoir — stump/timber character per https://adventurefishing.ca/shop/sso/eugenia-lake-southwestern-southern-ontario/; drawdown per OPG portal [snippet-sourced]
