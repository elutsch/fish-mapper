# Brand Voice — Lake Pages (Bite Club)

The voice is a **sharp local who actually fishes the lake, talking fast because they know their stuff** — punchy, playful, confident, comic-book energy. Not a brochure, not a textbook, not a hype reel. Specificity is still the product: if a sentence reads the same after you delete every proper noun and structure name, it is filler — energetic filler is still filler. Cut it.

## The register

- **Punchy.** Short sentences that hit. Vary the length — a couple of jabs, then a longer one to breathe. Rhythm is the whole game.
- **Verb-forward.** Fish *prowl, stack, bury, slide, crash, hug the rock.* Wind *fills in, pins you, flattens.* Lead with motion, not "there are."
- **Confident and declarative.** State the true thing like you know it, because the substrate backs you. No mush, no "can sometimes be found near."
- **Playful, dry, a little swagger.** A well-placed wink or a blunt truth lands. Wit, not clownery.
- **Fluent in angler.** Leeward shore, post-turnover, the bite shuts off, cabbage, flats, the drop. Insider language reads as both flavour and credibility.
- **Second person when it helps.** Talk to the angler at the ramp.

**The punch-vs-hype line:** punch comes from *how* you say a true, specific thing; hype comes from inflating a claim with no substance. Be loud about *specifics*, never about *superlatives you can't source*. Energy is free; claims are not. The non-negotiable rules below are the ceiling — punch never overrides them.

**Anti-repetition — vary or die:** no two species cards open the same way; rotate verbs (if pike "prowl," smallmouth don't also "prowl"); a fact stated once is spent — build on it, never restate it; kill crutch phrases repeated down the page ("relate to," "hold on," "known for").

**Where the voice goes quiet:** the Regulations snapshot. Exact, sourced, dated, disclaimered — no punch, no paraphrase, ever.

## Non-negotiable rules

1. **No brochure phrases.** Banned: "trophy waters," "angler's paradise," "hidden gem," "something for every angler," "world-class," "discover," "pristine," "stunning," "beautiful" (without a specific).
2. **No filler adverb openers.** Banned sentence-starters: "Naturally," "Notably," "Ideally," "Interestingly," "Of course."
3. **No unsourced superlatives / hedged superlatives.** No "possibly the best pike lake," "arguably the top," "known for big bass" — unless a cited quality signal supports it.
4. **No empty descriptors** ("beautiful," "scenic," "great fishing") without a concrete, named specific attached.
5. **No live / today conditions, ever.** No current water temp, wind, level, or "the bite is on." Pages are evergreen; the daily engine renders live data separately on top.
6. **No invented facts.** Every name, number, structure, claim, and regulation must already exist in the upstream verified inputs.
7. **No operators.** Charters, guides, and bait shops are out of scope — never named.
8. **Lead with the specific.** Open on a hook that carries a real specific — a named structure, a documented pattern, a blunt characterization of the fish, a season — then the why (habitat, forage, seasonal movement), then close on an actionable evergreen specific (a season window, a depth pattern, a structure type). Vary the opener card to card.
9. **No exclamation spam.** A rare, earned emphatic is fine; three per paragraph is hype.

## Tone tests
- Would a local angler recognize this as true and useful — and read it out loud without cringing? If not, cut or fix.
- Does every paragraph carry at least one proper noun or named structure? If not, it's filler.
- Does any card open the same way as another, or lean on a phrase used above it? Rewrite one.
- Did any today-reading slip in? Remove it.
- Is any line loud about nothing (superlative with no source, energy with no specific)? That's hype — cut it.
