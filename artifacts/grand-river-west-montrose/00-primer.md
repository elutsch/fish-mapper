# Lake Primer

## Physical character
A flowing reach of the Grand River at the West Montrose covered bridge in Woolwich Township — not a lake or reservoir but a wide, shallow river run. This is the transition zone where the cold tailwater below the Shand Dam gives way to warmwater river downstream.

## Morphology signal
River reach, not a basin — no surface area or depth-of-lake figures apply. The reach runs wide and shallow with a mix of faster riffles and rapids and slower flat "frog water," over a rock, gravel, and cobble bed. Water is running and generally clear-to-lightly-stained rather than a standing lake body.

## Watershed & access
Part of the upper–middle Grand River watershed, within Grand River Conservation Authority country; the Grand is a Canadian Heritage River. Public river access nearby is carry-in/wade-in (canoe, kayak, tube, wading angler), including a river access point at West Montrose; no trailer boat launch on this reach.

## Managing authorities
- Grand River Conservation Authority (GRCA)
- Township of Woolwich (local river access)
- Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources (FMZ 16 regulations)

## Fishery reputation signal
Warmwater river reach known first for smallmouth bass, with northern pike in the slower water and walleye and yellow perch also present; West Montrose is where the Grand's smallmouth water begins below the trout tailwater.
