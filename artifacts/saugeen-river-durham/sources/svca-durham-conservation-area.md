# Source cache — SVCA Durham Conservation Area

- URL: https://www.saugeenconservation.ca/outdoors-recreation/outdoor-spaces/durham/
- Accessed: 2026-07-20

## Key facts
- Managing authority: Saugeen Valley Conservation Authority (SVCA)
- Area: "over 61 ha (150 acres)"
- River frontage: "over 3 km of Saugeen River frontage"
- McGowan Falls: scenic cascade in the conservation area; named after Robert McGowan
- Dam: "The current dam provides a recreational reservoir upstream for swimming. It also helps in flood control for the town of Durham" (controls ice through winter into spring)
- Fishing "permitted within season only"; directs anglers to follow Ministry of Natural Resources and Forestry guidelines; no specific species listed
- Amenity list includes a "Boat ramp"
- Trails: five systems, ~4 km total — Cedar Ridge, McManus, Gunpit, Middle Dam, Riverview
- Day-use parking fee: $5.00/day (annual pass option); a widely cited figure is $5/day or $55/year
