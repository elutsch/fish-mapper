# Lake Primer

## Physical character
Man-made reservoir created to control flooding on Sixteen Mile Creek, sitting below the Niagara Escarpment just north of Highway 401 at Milton. Centrepiece of the Kelso Conservation Area.

## Morphology signal
Small reservoir with a shallow, warm-water character typical of a creek impoundment; also holds a swimming beach and paddling area. Suits warm-water species; no deep cold-water habitat signal found.

## Watershed & access
Part of the Sixteen Mile Creek watershed, managed by Conservation Halton within a day-use conservation area. Non-motorized boats only; shoreline fishing permitted except near the beach, boardwalk, waterpark, and boat-rental areas; small boat launch on site with park entry.

## Managing authorities
- Conservation Halton
- Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources (FMZ 16)

## Fishery reputation signal
Casual family fishery known for smallmouth bass, panfish, catfish, and carp, with some trout reported.
