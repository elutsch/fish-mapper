# Species Sub-Guides: Grand River at Brantford

## Smallmouth Bass
**Parent slug:** smallmouth-bass
**Tier:** Destination

### Lede
Rock, gravel, and moving water — that's the whole pitch. This reach is the downstream end of the Paris-to-Brantford "Exceptional Waters," a stretch the province wrote catch-and-release rules for specifically to protect the smallmouth. They hold on the current seams where riffles spill into runs and pile into the slack below Wilkes Dam. Most-logged fish on the Grand, and every one of them goes back.

### How it fishes
The reach reads like a ribbon of ambush points. Smallmouth key on the seams between fast and slow water — the tail of a riffle, the edge of a gravel bar, the soft water below Wilkes Dam where the dam breaks the gradient and concentrates fish. Cold water sliding in from Whitemans Creek and springs keeps the stretch honest through summer. The open window runs late spring through fall once the March-to-April sanctuary lifts; the artificial-lure, single-barbless-hook rule shapes how you fish it. Hook one in this current and it uses the flow against you — river smallmouth run and jump. Work upstream, cover the seams, and release clean. This is a fishery kept good on purpose, not by accident.

### Structure
- **Riffle-run-pool water over rock and gravel**: The core smallmouth habitat through the Paris-to-Brantford reach — fish hold on the current seams and the edges of gravel bars where riffles pour into runs.
- **Slack water and pools below Wilkes Dam**: The low-head dam breaks the river's gradient and concentrates fish in the softer water below it — a documented shore-and-canoe spot.
- **The Exceptional Waters reach (Paris to Brantford)**: Designated in part for its fishing quality; cold-water input from Whitemans Creek and springs helps sustain the fishery along the whole stretch.

### Rules for this species
- Season/limit: within the Grand River Paris-to-Brantford exception — Smallmouth Bass S-0 / C-0 (catch-and-release only); artificial lures only and 1 barbless hook only; fish sanctuary (no fishing) March 1 to the Friday before the fourth Saturday in April — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2025-12-08)
- Boundary: these rules apply upstream of the downtown Brantford pedestrian and service bridge; below it (Brantford to Lake Erie) FMZ 16 zone-wide bass limits apply — confirm which side you're on — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2025-12-08)
- Access: carry-in canoe/kayak or wade only — powerboats are prohibited (shallow); portage the marked route around Wilkes Dam — https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/conservation-areas/brant/ (effective 2026-07-20)

## Northern Pike
**Parent slug:** northern-pike
**Tier:** Strong

### Lede
Pike play the off-current game. Where the Grand slows below Wilkes Dam and weed lines up along the margins, they hang off the seam and wait for the river to deliver. The shore-and-canoe anglers at Brant Conservation Area target them right here. Second most-logged fish on this stretch — so it's less about whether they're present and more about reading the slack water.

### How it fishes
Find the water the current isn't fighting. The dam pool below Wilkes Dam is the anchor — slow, deep enough, and lined with the weedy margins pike bury into before they crash out on passing forage. The slower, wider water down toward the Brantford-to-Caledonia stretch offers the same setup: soft edges, weed, and ambush lanes. Perch and other panfish schooling the quieter pools are the menu. Pike are documented here as a shore-and-canoe target, so you don't need a boat to reach them — just the soft edges of the dam pool and the slower bends. Reach rules ride along: artificial lures, single barbless hook, and every pike goes back on this stretch.

### Structure
- **Slack water, pools, and weedy margins below Wilkes Dam**: A documented shore/canoe pike spot — the slow water off the current and the weedy edges give them ambush lanes.
- **Slower, wider water toward Brantford-to-Caledonia**: The varied-habitat, slow-moving stretch downstream holds pike along its soft edges and weed lines.

### Rules for this species
- Season/limit: within the Grand River Paris-to-Brantford exception — Northern Pike S-0 / C-0 (catch-and-release only); artificial lures only and 1 barbless hook only; fish sanctuary (no fishing) March 1 to the Friday before the fourth Saturday in April — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2025-12-08)
- Boundary: these rules apply upstream of the downtown Brantford pedestrian and service bridge; below it FMZ 16 zone-wide pike limits (S-6 / C-2, no size limit) apply — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2025-12-08)
- Access: carry-in / shore access via Brant Conservation Area, above or below Wilkes Dam; powerboats prohibited — https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/conservation-areas/brant/ (effective 2026-07-20)

## Walleye
**Parent slug:** walleye
**Tier:** Strong

### Lede
Depth and low light — that's the walleye read on this reach. They ride the deeper runs and the pool below Wilkes Dam, sliding shallow to feed when the sun's off the water. This is part of the lower-Grand walleye fishery that switches on at the May opener and rides through to the end of bass season in November. The dam break is the first place to look.

### How it fishes
Walleye work the seams and the deep. Daytime, they sit in the deeper runs and the pool below Wilkes Dam where the water carries some depth and colour; as the light drops, they move up onto the current edges to run down forage. The lower-Grand walleye fishery opens at the second-Saturday-of-May date and stays live through the November bass close, so the productive window is long — but the reach also sits under a spring sanctuary that shuts fishing entirely from March into April. Start at the dam pool, then work the deeper bends downstream. Low light does the heavy lifting: first and last hour move the most fish. And like everything on this stretch, walleye are catch-and-release — artificial lures and one barbless hook.

### Structure
- **Deeper runs and pools, and the slack water below Wilkes Dam**: The daytime holding water — walleye favour the deeper, lower-light water and the break in gradient the dam creates.
- **The reach as part of the lower-Grand walleye fishery**: A noted walleye stretch that opens in May and fishes through the November bass close.

### Rules for this species
- Season/limit: within the Grand River Paris-to-Brantford exception — Walleye S-0 / C-0 (catch-and-release only); artificial lures only and 1 barbless hook only; fish sanctuary (no fishing) March 1 to the Friday before the fourth Saturday in April — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2025-12-08)
- Boundary: these rules apply upstream of the downtown Brantford pedestrian and service bridge; below it FMZ 16 zone-wide walleye limits (S-4 / C-2, not more than 1 over 46 cm) apply — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2025-12-08)
- Access: carry-in canoe/kayak or shore/wading at Brant Conservation Area; powerboats prohibited; portage the marked route around Wilkes Dam — https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/conservation-areas/brant/ (effective 2026-07-20)
