# Species Sub-Guides: Laurel Creek Reservoir

## Largemouth Bass
**Parent slug:** largemouth-bass
**Tier:** Strong

### Lede
Timing is everything on a reservoir that empties every winter. From late fall the basin drops about 3.2 metres and most of the shallow stump-and-reed cover goes dry, and it doesn't refill to summer pool until spring. So the largemouth window is the wetted season: once the southwest corner's reedy shallows and the stump fields south of the island are underwater again, that flooded wood is where the fish live, straight through fall.

### How it fishes
This is a shallow, turbid, warm basin — sixty percent of it averages about a metre, and the deepest water only reaches a 1.5-to-3-metre band toward the dam. That plays to largemouth. They hold in the flooded cover: the emergent and submerged stumps south of the island, the reedy southwest corner, and the water weeds that fill the nutrient-rich basin. Forage is young panfish — pumpkinseed and rock bass — plus the minnows and dace that dominate the reservoir, all packed into the same weed and wood. Work it from the late-June bass opener through fall, while the reservoir sits at summer pool. Then respect the ceiling GRCA put on this water: the winter drawdown that dewaters the cover is why its management plan calls the fishery under-producing. No documented ice fishing exists here — the basin sits at its winter minimum, and the launch runs only May to mid-October.

### Structure
- **Stump fields south of the island**: Emergent and submerged stumps occupy most of the water south of the island, where depth is often under a metre — classic shallow wood cover, and prime largemouth water once it's wetted at summer pool.
- **Southwest-corner reedy shallows**: The reservoir's southwest corner holds shallow, reedy boundaries and numerous tree stumps — emergent vegetation plus wood, another cover magnet through the warm months.
- **Reservoir-wide submerged stumps**: GRCA warns paddlers of underwater tree stumps throughout the basin — scattered wood cover lake-wide, and a navigation hazard that worsens during drawdown.
- **Weed growth**: Abundant water weeds fill the warm, nutrient-enriched basin, adding the weed-edge cover largemouth relate to alongside the wood.
