# Species Taxonomy — Canonical Gamefish

Scope for this pipeline run: the six main gamefish across the FMZ 16 lake set. Every gamefish parent below is scored by Stage 1b, in the order listed. All carry `signatureRequiresEvidence: true` — a "Destination" tier requires named structure AND a citable quality signal.

Use these exact **display names** and **slugs** when naming species in any stage.

---

## Smallmouth Bass
- **slug:** `smallmouth-bass`
- **signatureRequiresEvidence:** true
- **Habitat:** Cool-to-warm water; needs clarity. Relates to hard structure — rocky main-lake points, mid-lake shoals, gravel/rubble flats, riprap, drop-offs. Poor fit for turbid, weed-choked, shallow-only water.
- **Forage:** Crayfish, perch, shiners, sculpins.
- **Eliminate when:** No rock/gravel structure and chronically turbid warm water.

## Largemouth Bass
- **slug:** `largemouth-bass`
- **signatureRequiresEvidence:** true
- **Habitat:** Warm, shallow, vegetated. Wants weed beds, lily pads, wood/timber, dock/laydown cover, back bays. Tolerates stained/eutrophic water and warm temps better than smallmouth.
- **Forage:** Sunfish, perch, frogs, crayfish, shiners.
- **Eliminate when:** Deep, cold, clear oligotrophic basin with no shallow weedy habitat.

## Northern Pike
- **slug:** `northern-pike`
- **signatureRequiresEvidence:** true
- **Habitat:** Cool water; ambush predator on weed edges, cabbage flats, weedy bays, inflows, timber. Needs vegetation for spring spawning. Widespread and tolerant.
- **Forage:** Perch, ciscoes, suckers, shiners, smaller gamefish.
- **Eliminate when:** No vegetated bays or weed structure and no documented presence.

## Walleye
- **slug:** `walleye`
- **signatureRequiresEvidence:** true
- **Habitat:** Cool water, low-light feeder. Favours larger/deeper lakes and reservoirs with some stain or turbidity; rocky reefs, main-lake points, gravel bars, current areas, river inflows. Needs clean gravel/rubble or river current for spawning.
- **Forage:** Perch, ciscoes, shiners.
- **Eliminate when:** Small, clear, warm, weedy pond with no deep or stained water and no documented/stocked presence.

## Black Crappie
- **slug:** `black-crappie`
- **signatureRequiresEvidence:** true
- **Habitat:** Warm, clear-to-lightly-stained water; relates to submerged wood, brush, weed edges, and standing timber; suspends over basins seasonally. Panfish gamefish.
- **Forage:** Zooplankton, insect larvae, small minnows, young-of-year fish.
- **Eliminate when:** Cold, deep, structureless water with no cover and no documented presence.

## Yellow Perch
- **slug:** `yellow-perch`
- **signatureRequiresEvidence:** true
- **Habitat:** Widespread and tolerant; schools over weed edges, flats, and mid-depth basins. Both a gamefish (panfish) and a primary forage species for pike and walleye — note it under forage when relevant.
- **Forage:** Invertebrates, insect larvae, small minnows, fish eggs.
- **Eliminate when:** Rarely absent where the lake supports any panfish; mark Absent only if habitat is clearly wrong or presence is undocumented.

---

## Taxonomy order (use for scoring and card ordering)
1. Smallmouth Bass
2. Largemouth Bass
3. Northern Pike
4. Walleye
5. Black Crappie
6. Yellow Perch
