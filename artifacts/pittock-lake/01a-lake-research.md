# Lake Research: Pittock Lake (Gordon Pittock Reservoir)

## In-lake structure (by species)
Documented, named structure is modest for this reservoir — no public bathymetric chart was found. What is documented:

### Smallmouth Bass
- Constructed river-stone shoals and gravel spawning beds (300 tonnes of river stone, placed Oct 2016 by UTRCA near shore in the reservoir) — purpose-built spawning/refuge structure for bass and perch; hard rock in an otherwise soft-bottomed impoundment — https://www.heartfm.ca/news/local-news/pittock-conservation-area-fish-habitat/
- 125 m of wooden cover structures ("underwater shelves"/cavity cover, 2016 UTRCA project) — engineered wood cover holding bass — https://www.heartfm.ca/news/local-news/pittock-conservation-area-fish-habitat/
- Pittock Dam (west end) basin — the dam-end pool is the reservoir's deep water (dam discharges "cooler water from reservoir depths" in summer); dam area is a heavily fished feature per angler videos — http://thamesriver.on.ca/water-management/flood-control-structures/pittock-dam/ ; https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4QfKeL10rVY (source: angler report — user-contributed)

### Largemouth Bass
- 50 ha cattail marsh at the upstream (east) end of the reservoir — the only large documented vegetated shallow; classic warm-water cover — https://www.ibacanada.org/site.jsp?siteID=ON027

- Upstream shallow arm east of the campground (20 km/h restricted zone toward Oxford Road 4 on the boating map) — shallow, riverine upper end; quieter water by rule — https://www.pittockconservationarea.ca/_files/ugd/5600a4_4a52661f11b84c61bc3952d10a9a03c3.pdf

## Seasonal patterns
- Best seasons by species: Pike — open-water spring (post-May opener) and again pre-freeze-up; also through ice (angler-reported). Bass — from the late-June opener through fall; the reservoir is drawn down progressively from mid-summer, concentrating fish toward the main west basin (drawdown documented; concentration effect is a general reservoir pattern). Perch — year-round season incl. ice (angler-reported ice jigging) — drawdown: https://thamesriver.on.ca/parks-recreation-natural-areas/boating/ ; ice activity: (source: angler report — user-contributed, YouTube/search results)
- Spawn windows (general regional FMZ 16 patterns, not lake-documented): pike — ice-out/early spring in flooded vegetation (the east-end cattail marsh is the documented habitat of this type); bass — late spring/early summer (season opens 4th Sat June to protect it, per FMZ 16 season structure); walleye — early spring (closed Mar 16–mid May zone-wide); carp — documented spawning in May at Pittock (2021 die-off article) — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 ; https://www.heartfm.ca/news/local-news/utrca-removes-over-600-dead-fish-from-pittock-lake/
- Reservoir cycle (the dominant seasonal driver): fills to summer conservation level in spring; lowered from mid-June onward; by August drawdown exposes mudflats "almost daily"; winter holding (lowest) level by early December through winter — https://thamesriver.on.ca/parks-recreation-natural-areas/boating/ ; https://www.ibacanada.org/site.jsp?siteID=ON027
- Summer water quality: algal blooms (incl. toxic blue-green) possible May–Sept in hot, still, nutrient-rich conditions; bloom decomposition can cause localized hypoxia — https://thamesriver.on.ca/parks-recreation-natural-areas/fishing/algal-blooms/
- Turnover/thermocline: not formally documented; dam can draw cooler bottom water in summer (some depth-related thermal structure at the dam end) — http://thamesriver.on.ca/water-management/flood-control-structures/pittock-dam/
- Ice fishing: yellow perch and pike through the ice are angler-reported (YouTube/search results); reservoir is at its lowest winter level then and CA motorized-boating services are closed (May–Oct season) — (source: angler report — user-contributed) ; https://thamesriver.on.ca/parks-recreation-natural-areas/boating/

## Notable facts (defensible claims)
- UTRCA (with partners) completed a purpose-built fish-habitat project in the reservoir in Oct 2016: 300 tonnes of river stone for shoals and gravel spawning beds, 125 m of wooden cover structures, plus shoreline/aquatic planting — targeting bass, pike, perch, walleye and pumpkinseed — https://www.heartfm.ca/news/local-news/pittock-conservation-area-fish-habitat/
- Pittock Dam was built 1964–1967 (~$6M) for flood control and summer low-flow augmentation of the Thames through Woodstock and downstream to London — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pittock_Dam ; http://thamesriver.on.ca/water-management/flood-control-structures/pittock-dam/
- The reservoir is 10.3 km long with ~452 ha summer surface area and a 50 ha cattail marsh at its upstream end; it is a designated Important Bird Area (ON027) famous for fall waterbird concentrations on drawdown mudflats — https://www.ibacanada.org/site.jsp?siteID=ON027
- Northern pike is the most-reported catch on the reservoir among Fishbrain users (99 members logging pike vs 70 smallmouth, 52 largemouth) — https://fishbrain.com/fishing-waters/k_ja9BgJ/pittock-reservoir (source: angler report — user-contributed)
- Pittock Conservation Area is a ~2,000-acre recreation area with 250+ campsites on the north shore — a rare camp-and-fish reservoir minutes from Woodstock — https://www.pittockconservationarea.ca/ (via search results) ; https://www.pittockconservationarea.ca/day-use-activities

## Gotchas & disambiguation
- **Spring sanctuary at the dam:** Thames River from the Pittock dam downstream to the Highway 59 bridge is a no-fishing sanctuary Mar 15 → Friday before the 2nd Saturday in May. Anglers fishing "the Dam" in spring can be offside — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16
- **VHS (Viral Hemorrhagic Septicemia) is present in the Thames River system** — noted on the Pittock CA site; baitfish movement rules apply — https://www.pittockconservationarea.ca/day-use-activities
- **Managed drawdown:** levels drop from mid-June to early December; late-summer/fall launching can be limited and no-wake restrictions have been imposed due to low water; a boat pass does not guarantee season-long access — https://thamesriver.on.ca/parks-recreation-natural-areas/boating/ ; https://www.facebook.com/PittockCA/ (low-water no-wake posts, via search results)
- **Blue-green algal blooms** possible May–Sept; avoid contact when water looks like "pea soup"/spilled paint — https://thamesriver.on.ca/parks-recreation-natural-areas/fishing/algal-blooms/
- **Motorized launch is north shore only (fee/permit, dawn–dusk, no PWC)**; south shore (Roth Park) is carry-in/non-motorized — https://www.pittockconservationarea.ca/day-use-activities ; https://www.tourismoxford.ca/listing/roth-park/369/
- **Name confusion:** "Pittock Lake", "Pittock Reservoir", "Gordon Pittock Reservoir", and "the Pittock Dam" all refer to this waterbody; distinct from Pittock Dam tailwater (Thames River reaches, different regs incl. the sanctuary) — https://www.anglersatlas.com/place/728882/gordon-pittock-reservoir
- **Surface-area discrepancy:** 452 ha (IBA, summer pool) vs 209 ha (Anglers Atlas) — treat 452 ha summer figure as the better-provenanced number; the difference is consistent with the large seasonal drawdown — URLs above
- Water at the CA is not health-unit tested for swimming — https://www.pittockconservationarea.ca/day-use-activities
