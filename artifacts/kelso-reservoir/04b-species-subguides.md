# Species Sub-Guides: Kelso Reservoir

## Largemouth Bass
**Parent slug:** largemouth-bass
**Tier:** Strong

### Lede
The northwest end is where Kelso's largemouth story starts — shallow, weedy water where Sixteen Mile Creek slides in and the flats warm first. This is a bass reservoir by default: about 30 hectares, warm and shallow top to bottom, exactly the habitat green bass were built for. From the beach-and-inflow flats to Pirate's Cove to the boardwalk margins, the whole shoreline reads as one long cover map. Fish it like one.

### How it fishes
Bass season opens the fourth Saturday of June and runs to November 30, and at Kelso that whole stretch is a shallow-water game. The reservoir has no real deep-cold refuge — it's a warm creek impoundment start to finish — so the fish stay tied to cover and weed edges rather than chasing a thermocline. Work Pirate's Cove and the northwest flats early, when the shallowest water warms first, then follow the shade along the boardwalk margins as summer settles in. Largemouth here eat what the reservoir grows: sunfish, perch, and crayfish along the vegetated edges. One local wrinkle — Conservation Halton permits worms as the only live bait, no minnows or crayfish — so plan around soft plastics and worms rather than a livebait forage match.

### Structure
- **"Pirate's Cove" shallow jut**: A named shallow cove off the middle of the reservoir — weedy, warm, and shallow, the kind of back-pocket cover largemouth hold in through the season.
- **Boardwalk shoreline margins**: A boardwalk lines one shore; fishing off the boardwalk itself is prohibited, but the shaded, dock-like margin beside it is documented shoreline cover — most of the reservoir's largemouth get logged along water like this.
- **Shallow weedy flats at the beach/inflow (northwest) end**: The warm shallow head of the impoundment where Sixteen Mile Creek enters; community catch logs are dominated by largemouth and pumpkinseed out of this end.

### Rules for this species
- Season: fourth Saturday in June to November 30 — Limit: S-6 / C-2 (combined with Smallmouth Bass) — Size: none — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2025-12-08)
- Waterbody exceptions: none — Kelso Reservoir is not listed in the FMZ 16 exceptions or sanctuaries; zone-wide regulations apply — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2025-12-08)
- Park rules (Conservation Halton, not provincial law): worms are the only permitted live bait (no minnows, crayfish, or frogs); lead tackle is discouraged; no fishing off the beach, boardwalk, pump house and its lot, Kelso Cove Waterpark, the boat-rentals beach, or the dam — https://www.conservationhalton.ca/activities/fishing/ (effective 2026-07-18)

## Yellow Perch
**Parent slug:** yellow-perch
**Tier:** Strong

### Lede
Kelso's perch don't take a season off — the fishery is open year-round on paper, and in the water it's the most dependable catch a shore angler will find here. The boardwalk shoreline margins are the everyman spot: reachable on foot, holding schoolfish through the warm months. Want bigger water? The mid-lake basin sits deeper off the middle for anyone in a canoe. Either way, they school where the food is, and they school in numbers.

### How it fishes
Perch school, so finding one usually means finding a pile. Along the boardwalk shore and the shallow margins they hold over the weed edges and flats, picking off invertebrates, insect larvae, and small minnows — light-tackle, small-hook fishing that suits kids and beginners. The season never closes, but the practical window is the park's own: Kelso runs day-use from roughly May through October, so that's when shore access lines up with the bite. For a change of scale, the mid-lake basin holds fish out over deeper water — reachable from a canoe or kayak, since the reservoir is non-motorized with electric trolling motors the only exception. Perch double as the reservoir's forage base, which is part of why the bass fishing holds up.

### Structure
- **Boardwalk and shoreline margins**: Shore-accessible edges along the boardwalk shore where perch school through the warm months — the most community-confirmed catch on the reservoir and the easiest fish to reach on foot.
- **Mid-lake basin**: The deeper water off the middle of the reservoir (anglers report roughly 20 feet); perch have been taken here over the open basin, reachable from a non-motorized boat.

### Rules for this species
- Season: open all year — Limit: S-50 / C-25 — Size: none — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2025-12-08)
