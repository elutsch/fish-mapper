# Species Sub-Guides: Grand River at Paris

## Smallmouth Bass
**Parent slug:** smallmouth-bass
**Tier:** Destination

### Lede
The Paris-to-Brantford reach is smallmouth water first and everything else second. It carries the province's "Exceptional Waters" tag for a reason: an extended season and barbless, artificial-only, catch-and-release rules built to protect the bass. You wade it — hip-to-chest over rock, gravel, and rubble — reading the current seams and pool tail-outs where river bronzebacks live.

### How it fishes
The pattern is current, not depth. Smallmouth hold where fast water shoulders into slow — the seams, the pockets behind rock, the tail-outs where pools dump into the next riffle — and they run down crayfish and sculpins across the gravel and rubble bottom. When the reach heats up in summer, the cold water bleeding in from Whitemans Creek and the springs pulls fish onto those inflow seams. As the shallow runs warm through, they slide to the deeper holes above Wilkes Dam in Brantford, where rubble and old-dam structure add depth. Best summer through early fall. Every bass goes back — this is a release-only reach.

### Structure
- **The wadeable Exceptional Waters reach (rock/gravel/rubble)**: the run-pool-riffle river itself between Paris and Brantford, hip-to-chest deep, is the core smallmouth habitat and the water the special catch-and-release regulation was built to protect.
- **Current seams and pool tail-outs**: where faster water meets slower holding water over gravel and rubble — the classic river-smallmouth ambush lies in a reach averaging about a metre deep.
- **Deep holes above Wilkes Dam (Brantford)**: rubble and old-dam remnants create depth and structure that hold smallmouth when the shallow upstream runs warm.
- **Whitemans Creek confluence**: a cold-water inflow between Paris and Brantford that creates a summer temperature refuge and a feeding lane.

### Rules for this species
- Reach special regulation: catch-and-release only (S-0 / C-0), artificial lures only, single barbless hook — from 100 m below the Hwy 2 bridge in Paris to the pedestrian bridge above Brant Conservation Area, Brantford — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2025-12-08)
- Base FMZ 16 bass season: fourth Saturday in June to Nov 30 — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2025-12-08)
- Sanctuary closures on this reach: no fishing Oct 1–Nov 30 (Penman Dam to William Street Bridge) and March 1 to the Friday before the fourth Saturday in April (Paris to Brantford pedestrian bridge) — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2025-12-08)

## Northern Pike
**Parent slug:** northern-pike
**Tier:** Strong

### Lede
Pike are the soft-water fish on a river that mostly moves. Where the Grand slows — the deeper pools, the slack margins, the millpond backing up behind Penman's Dam — a river pike sets up on the current break and waits. The deep water above Wilkes Dam is the dependable address, cool-season best, and every fish here goes back under the reach's release-only rule.

### How it fishes
Look for slack, not speed. In the wadeable Paris-to-Brantford water, pike hold in the slower runs and deeper pools and along the soft edges where current dies, ambushing baitfish that drift down the seam. The deep water above Wilkes Dam in Brantford gives them the depth and structure they want in a shallow river; the slack millpond above Penman's Dam in Paris offers the same soft holding water upstream. Cold water suits them, so spring and fall are the windows — the reach's cold-water inflows keep temperatures workable. Artificial lures and barbless hooks only, and pike are catch-and-release here.

### Structure
- **Slower runs, deep pools, and the deep water above Wilkes Dam**: the reach's holding water, where a river pike ambushes from current breaks and slack margins with enough depth to hold.
- **Slack backwaters / the millpond above Penman's Dam (Paris)**: soft-current water where the river pools behind the dam — pike-friendly holding water upstream in the reach.

### Rules for this species
- Reach special regulation: catch-and-release only (S-0 / C-0), artificial lures only, single barbless hook (Paris to Brantford boundary) — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2025-12-08)
- Base FMZ 16 pike season: Jan 1–Mar 31 and second Saturday in May to Dec 31; S-6 / C-2, no size limit (harvest overridden to C-0 in this reach) — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2025-12-08)
- Sanctuary closures: no fishing Oct 1–Nov 30 (Penman Dam to William Street Bridge) and March 1 to the Friday before the fourth Saturday in April (Paris to Brantford pedestrian bridge) — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2025-12-08)

## Walleye
**Parent slug:** walleye
**Tier:** Strong

### Lede
Walleye hold where the Grand gives them depth and cool water. The GRCA credits the cold flow bleeding in from Whitemans Creek and the springs for keeping them in this reach, and they sit in the current seams and the dim, deeper water. The holes above Wilkes Dam are the anchor — best worked at dawn and dusk, and every fish released.

### How it fishes
Fish the low light and the cool water. Walleye in the Paris-to-Brantford reach favour the deeper, dimmer water and the current areas where the cold inflow from Whitemans Creek and the springs holds temperatures down. The deep holes above Wilkes Dam in Brantford — rubble and old-dam structure — are the reliable holding water, letting fish sit off the light and slide up the seams to feed as the sun drops. Spring and fall, worked at dawn and dusk, are the prime windows. As with the bass and pike, walleye here are catch-and-release, on artificial lures and a single barbless hook.

### Structure
- **Deep water and holes above Wilkes Dam (Brantford)**: rubble and old-dam structure create the depth that concentrates walleye in an otherwise shallow river.
- **Current areas and cold-water inflow near Whitemans Creek**: the cooler, oxygenated flowing water the GRCA credits for supporting walleye through the reach.

### Rules for this species
- Reach special regulation: catch-and-release only (S-0 / C-0), artificial lures only, single barbless hook (Paris to Brantford boundary) — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2025-12-08)
- Base FMZ 16 walleye season: Jan 1–Mar 15 and second Saturday in May to Dec 31; S-4 / C-2, not more than 1 greater than 46 cm (harvest overridden to C-0 in this reach) — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2025-12-08)
