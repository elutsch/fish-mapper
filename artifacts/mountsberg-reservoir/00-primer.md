# Lake Primer

## Physical character
Reservoir on Bronte Creek behind the Mountsberg Dam near Campbellville, within Mountsberg Conservation Area. Flood-control impoundment that doubles as a wildlife and waterfowl area.

## Morphology signal
Small-to-mid-sized reservoir, shallow and weedy throughout — warm-water habitat rather than deep, cold water. Marshy, vegetated character consistent with its role as a wildlife sanctuary.

## Watershed & access
Part of the Bronte Creek watershed, managed by Conservation Halton as a conservation-area reservoir. Shore fishing and hand-launched, non-motorized boats only, with access through the conservation area; live bait is not permitted per the conservation authority.

## Managing authorities
- Conservation Halton
- Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources (FMZ 16)

## Fishery reputation signal
Shallow weedy reservoir known for largemouth bass, with northern pike, black crappie, pumpkinseed, and brown bullhead also present.
