## Notable facts (defensible claims)
- Wildwood Dam (built 1962–1965) was among the UTRCA's first major flood-control works; during the February 2018 flood its operations cut Trout Creek's peak flow from 65 m³/s to 21 m³/s (a 68% reduction), helping keep the North Thames below the St. Marys floodwall — https://thamesriver.on.ca/wp-content/uploads/WildwoodDam_QA-June2019.pdf
- In dry summers, releases from Wildwood Reservoir supply 25–50% (or more) of the flow in the North Thames River — https://thamesriver.on.ca/water-management/flood-control-structures/wildwood-dam/
- The reservoir is one of only three large boatable UTRCA reservoirs (with Fanshawe and Pittock), and the UTRCA promotes all three as perch/bass/pike fisheries — https://thamesriver.on.ca/parks-recreation-natural-areas/fishing/
- 370 angler catches logged on Fishbrain with pike (88 anglers) and smallmouth bass (80) leading — a meaningful volume of user-contributed catch evidence for a lake this size — https://fishbrain.com/fishing-waters/dW0o2uVF/wildwood-lake (angler report — user-contributed)
- Wildwood is a designated Important Bird Area, noted for large gull concentrations in fall and early winter — https://www.kayakfamily.ca/kayak-wildwood-lake-conservation-area.html
- Wildwood Conservation Area is a 3,200-acre property with ~386–425 campsites and ~30 km of trails around the reservoir — https://ontarioconservationareas.ca/conservation-areas/wildwood/

## Gotchas & disambiguation
- Name confusion: "Wildwood Lake" exists in Minnesota, California (Lake Wildwood), New Jersey, and elsewhere — onX and several search hits returned the Minnesota lake. This lake is the UTRCA's Wildwood Reservoir/Wildwood Lake on Trout Creek between Stratford and St. Marys, Ontario (43.2519, -81.0376) — https://www.anglersatlas.com/place/728791/wildwood-lake
- Drawdown hazard: annual ~3.5 m swing (6.55 m → 3 m). Late-summer/fall boaters can lose access to arms and eventually the launch; a season boat pass does not guarantee season-long reservoir access — https://thamesriver.on.ca/parks-recreation-natural-areas/boating/ ; https://thamesriver.on.ca/wp-content/uploads/WildwoodDam_QA-June2019.pdf
- No personal watercraft on any UTRCA reservoir; no-wake and restricted-access zones may be imposed — https://thamesriver.on.ca/parks-recreation-natural-areas/boating/
- Bait transport: Southern Bait Management Zone rules apply (no moving live/dead baitfish or leeches across BMZ boundaries) — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16
- Downstream regs differ: Trout Creek below the dam and the Thames system carry their own FMZ 16 exceptions; do not extend reservoir rules to the creek — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16
- The watershed figure differs between UTRCA pages: dam page says the facility drains 141 km²; the 2019 Q&A gives 162 km² for the full Trout Creek watershed — cite carefully — https://thamesriver.on.ca/water-management/flood-control-structures/wildwood-dam/ ; https://thamesriver.on.ca/wp-content/uploads/WildwoodDam_QA-June2019.pdf
