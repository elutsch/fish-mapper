- Waterbody exception (upper Grand special-regs section) — The FMZ 16 exceptions define an artificial-lure-only, single-barbless-hook, trout catch-and-release (brook/brown/rainbow S-0/C-0) section on the upper Grand whose most downstream sub-reach runs "between 100 metres downstream of Pilkington Township and Woolwich Township boundary line and 100 metres upstream of Highway 86 bridge." The Highway 86 bridge is UPSTREAM of the West Montrose covered bridge, so the covered-bridge reach lies below this section and follows zone defaults. Anglers fishing upstream toward the Highway 86 bridge should confirm which side of that line they are on, because the artificial-lure and single-barbless-hook restrictions apply above it. — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16
- Sanctuary / closure — None on this reach. (The Grand River fish sanctuary — no fishing Mar 1 to the Friday before the fourth Saturday in April, plus S-0/C-0 for pike, smallmouth, walleye, and trout — applies to the Paris-to-Brantford reach far downstream, NOT West Montrose.) — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16
- Bait note — FMZ 16 is within the Southern Bait Management Zone, which restricts transport of live/dead baitfish and leeches. — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16

## Notable facts (defensible claims)
- West Montrose is the transition point where the Grand's cold brown-trout tailwater gives way to warmwater river — smallmouth bass become the dominant gamefish here and continue downstream. — https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/fishing/fishing-rivers-and-streams/
- The reach runs under the West Montrose Covered Bridge — built 1880–1881 by John Bear, ~205 ft / 62 m over two spans of oak and white pine — the only remaining historic covered bridge in Ontario ("Kissing Bridge"), a Provincial Historic Site (1960) with Ontario Heritage Act designation (2007). — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Montrose_Covered_Bridge

- A public carry-in river access point (#204) opened at West Montrose (West Montrose Family Campground, 6344 Line 86), open mid-May to mid-October. — https://www.woolwich.ca/news/posts/grand-opening-of-a-new-river-access-point-in-west-montrose/

## Gotchas & disambiguation
- Regulatory boundary runs through the area: the upper-Grand artificial-lure-only / single-barbless-hook / trout catch-and-release section ends "100 metres upstream of Highway 86 bridge," which is just UPSTREAM of the covered bridge. The covered-bridge reach itself is zone-default warmwater; anglers working up toward the Highway 86 bridge must confirm which side of the line they are on. — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16
- Name confusion: multiple "Grand River" fisheries exist (e.g., Grand River in Michigan/Ohio). This is the Grand River of southern Ontario (Canadian Heritage River, Lake Erie watershed) at West Montrose — do not import Michigan/Ohio Grand River structure or regs. — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Montrose_Covered_Bridge
- Access is carry-in / wade only — no trailer boat launch on this reach; the reach can be too low to paddle in summer. — https://ontariotroutandsteelhead.com/fishing-the-grand-river-ontario/ (source: guide report — user-contributed)
- Bait restriction: FMZ 16 is in the Southern Bait Management Zone — baitfish/leech transport restrictions apply. — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16
