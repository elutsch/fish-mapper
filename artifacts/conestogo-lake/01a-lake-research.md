## Seasonal patterns
- Best season(s) by species: Late spring through summer is the documented peak — GRCA/angler reports flag weedlines and points producing best late spring–summer for pike, walleye, and smallmouth. — https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/fishing/fishing-grand-river-conservation-areas/
- Spawn windows (general regional pattern, not lake-specific measurement): walleye spawn early spring on river-inflow gravel/current at the arm heads; pike spawn earliest, in flooded/weedy shallows of the arms; smallmouth spawn late spring–early June on rock/gravel; crappie in the weedy arms in late spring; bass season opens fourth Saturday of June (post-spawn) per regs. (general regional pattern)
- Turnover / thermocline notes: as a flood-control reservoir that releases cold water from depth at the dam, the main basin toward the dam likely stratifies in summer with a cool hypolimnion; the shallow stained upper arms warm faster. — https://www.grandriver.ca/our-watershed/fisheries-management/tailwater-fisheries/
- Ice fishing: Walleye (Jan 1–Mar 15) and pike (Jan 1–Mar 31) have winter open seasons in FMZ 16, and crappie/perch are open all year, so a hard-water fishery is legally available; however, reservoir drawdown and fluctuating levels create unstable/hazardous ice — no specific documented ice-fishing access found. — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16
