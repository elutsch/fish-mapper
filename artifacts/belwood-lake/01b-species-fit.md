# Species Fit: Belwood Lake

**FMZ note:** Taxonomy assigned FMZ 16; Stage 1a verified this against the official Ontario MNR FMZ 16 source and multiple waterbody databases. CONFIRMED — no discrepancy to flag.

## Summary
- **Destination species:** Northern Pike
- **Strong species:** Smallmouth Bass
- **Headline structure surfaced:** Islands and rocky main-body shorelines and the northern narrows into the Grand River channel (smallmouth); weed edges/flats plus the drowned river channel / deep former riverbed (pike, walleye); steep drops into the main basin at winter drawdown (perch).

Note: No official MNR fisheries assessment or netting CPUE exists for the reservoir, and Fish ON-Line shows no stocking record. The only citable quality signal available is the long-running Belwood & District Lions Pike Derby (a documented tournament history for pike and walleye). This caps most species that have named structure but no quality signal at Strong or below.

## Scoring detail

### Smallmouth Bass
- **Tier:** Strong
- **Structure surfaced:** Islands and rocky shorelines through the main body (named bass hotspots); the northern narrows where the lake pinches into the Grand River channel; bedrock/boulder substrate in the upper reservoir (crayfish habitat, per 1977 survey).
- **Evidence signal:** Named rocky structure across multiple sources plus second-most-logged species; no MNR survey CPUE, documented trophy, tournament, or stocking record — so capped at Strong.
- **Reasoning:** Genuine rock-structure smallmouth fishery with crayfish forage and a real reason to target here, but murky/stained water is a mild habitat drag and there is no citable quality signal to support Destination.

### Northern Pike
- **Tier:** Destination
- **Structure surfaced:** Weed edges and flats (primary ambush cover); the deep drowned river channel and the northern section into the Grand River channel; shoreline/island edges.
- **Evidence signal:** Long-running annual Belwood & District Lions Pike Derby, a pike-and-walleye tournament run with the GRCA, "All Fish must be caught in Belwood Lake above the Shand Dam," length-based C&R with a 20" pike minimum — a documented tournament history (source archives span 2015–2025; specific edition number unverified). Pike is also the lake's best-known and most-logged species (157 Fishbrain catches, top of the list).
- **Reasoning:** Named ambush structure plus a citable multi-decade tournament history and a clear reputational lead make pike the one species people genuinely travel to Belwood to target.
- **Sources:** https://www.pikederby.org/newpage ; https://lakebelwood.com/things-to-do/adventure/link-to-fishing-page/ ; https://www.thefishinguide.com/belwood-lake.php ; https://fishbrain.com/fishing-waters/91L2cSz5/lake-belwood

### Walleye
- **Tier:** Present
- **Structure surfaced:** Deep drowned river channel; edge of drowned trees over rock and drop-off transitions (angler-report, user-contributed).
- **Evidence signal:** Named as a co-target in the Lions derby (18⅛" minimum), but abundance is anecdotally low — anglers describe a small-sized population that is "a bit of a mystery," and the structure detail is largely user-contributed.
- **Reasoning:** Documented presence and a derby signal, but low density, small fish, and no official abundance data mean it is not a reliable reason to fish here — held at Present rather than over-tiered.
