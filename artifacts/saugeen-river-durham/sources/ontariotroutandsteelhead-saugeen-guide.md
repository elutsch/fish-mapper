## Key facts
- Upper Saugeen (around Durham): trout water. "Above the dam in Durham is mostly brook trout, however, there are some brown trout mixed in."
- Upper river averages "15 to 25 feet in width"; below the Rocky Saugeen tributary it widens to "over 50 feet wide" and is "teeming with brown trout, brook trout, and resident rainbow trout."
- Habitat: "deep pools, fast runs," challenging rapids and serene meadow stretches in the upper sections.
- Brown trout can reach "up to 30 inches."
- Warmwater species (smallmouth bass, pike, musky) become prevalent in the LOWER river: "Summer brings excellent fishing for smallmouth bass, pike, and musky from Walkerton downstream" — Walkerton is well below Durham.
- Lower river primary bass forage: crayfish; trophy smallmouth 17"–21" common in the lower reaches.
- The upper trout sections near Durham are NOT characterized as smallmouth bass habitat; bass fishing is downstream from Walkerton.
