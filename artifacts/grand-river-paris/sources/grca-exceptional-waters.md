# CACHE — GRCA Exceptional Waters (Paris to Brantford)

URL: https://www.grandriver.ca/our-watershed/fisheries-management/exceptional-waters
Accessed: 2026-07-20

- The Grand River between Paris and Brantford is designated "Exceptional Waters."
- "Cold water entering the river from Whitemans Creek and springs makes it a good habitat for smallmouth bass, walleye, northern pike and rainbow trout." Supports species at risk: black redhorse and river redhorse.
- River valley features Carolinian forest.
- "The fishing season was extended, but special regulations are in place that make this a barbless hook, artificial lure, and catch and release zone."
- Access improvements: Bean Park (Paris), Brant Park, Cockshutt Bridge.
- Two GRCA rail trails run the corridor (Cambridge to Paris Rail Trail / SC Johnson Trail).

Related:
- GRCA Fishing Rivers and Streams: https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/fishing/fishing-rivers-and-streams/ — "Exceptional Waters Reach"; species smallmouth bass, pike, crappie, carp, sunfish, rock bass, walleye; wadeable (avg depth ~1 m / 3 ft Brantford up to Paris/Cambridge); good for wading, canoe, drift boat.
- Whitemans Creek joins the Lower Grand between Paris and Brantford; cold-water tributary; own special regs (artificial lures, barbless).
