# Laurel Creek Reservoir

## Overview

Laurel Creek Reservoir is a shallow, turbid urban impoundment — 101 hectares of flood-control water held behind a 1966 dam on Laurel Creek, tucked inside Laurel Creek Conservation Area in Waterloo. Sixty percent of the basin averages about a metre deep; the deepest water, toward the dam, only reaches a 1.5-to-3-metre band. Laurel Creek is a Grand River tributary, and this is a working GRCA flood reservoir — drawn down roughly 3.2 metres every winter, which dewaters most of the shallow cover until spring and is why GRCA rates the fishery limited. Largemouth bass are the fish that fit: warm, weedy, stump-filled shallows are exactly their water, and the basin is thick with submerged and emergent stumps, especially south of the island. Smallmouth are here too, but the turbid, rock-free shallows fight them. Your way in is the gravel carry-in launch off Westmount Road North — paddle or electric trolling motor only, no power boats, open May through mid-October.

## Notable Facts
- The dam was completed in 1966; the reservoir was built for flood control, low-flow augmentation, and pollution abatement on Laurel Creek, a Grand River tributary. — https://uwaterloo.ca/global-engagement-seminar/past-seminars/who-laurel-creek/case-study-laurel-creek-watershed
- Laurel is one of only seven large multi-purpose flood-control reservoirs operated by the GRCA across the Grand River watershed. — https://www.grandriver.ca/our-watershed/river-data/reservoir-levels/
- GRCA's own management plan assesses the fishery as under-producing due to water level fluctuations (winter drawdown). — https://www.grandriver.ca/media/aqohbnr1/managementplan_laurel.pdf
- GRCA's public fishing page lists the fishery as "Carp, bullhead" and characterizes fishing as limited. — https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/fishing/fishing-grand-river-conservation-areas/
- Not a stocked fishery — no records in either official MNRF stocking dataset; the warm-water community is self-sustaining. — https://services1.arcgis.com/TJH5KDher0W13Kgo/arcgis/rest/services/FishStockingDataForRecreationalPurposes/FeatureServer/0/query

## Largemouth Bass
**Tier:** Strong
**Best Season:** Late-June opener through fall, at summer pool
**Structure:** stump fields south of the island; southwest-corner reedy shallows; reservoir-wide submerged stumps; weed growth

Largemouth own the stump water. South of the island the reservoir turns into a flooded stump field under a metre of water, and the southwest corner piles on reedy shallows and more submerged wood — warm, turbid, weed-lined cover with a bass parked in it. Fish it once the basin fills to summer pool and the stumps are wetted, from the late-June opener through fall. In water this shallow and this uniform, the wood is the whole game: work the thickest emergent stumps and the reed edges, and skip the open flats.

Also swimming around down there: Smallmouth Bass, holding what little deeper water the reservoir has toward the dam — though the turbid, rock-free shallows are stacked against them.
