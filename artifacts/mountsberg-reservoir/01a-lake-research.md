## In-lake structure (by species)

### Largemouth Bass
- Dense weedbeds and weedless pockets on the heavily overgrown (marshy) side of the reservoir — "the really overgrown side has some nice fish in the small weedless pockets" — (source: angler report — user-contributed, ontariofishingforums.com search snippet)
- Shoreline along the CP rail line that crosses the reservoir — evening topwater bass ("about 5 nice bass using a top water Rapala Popper in the evening" along the train tracks) — (source: angler report — user-contributed, ontariofishingforums.com search snippet); the rail crossing itself is documented at https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mountsberg_Conservation_Area — note anglers warn being on the rail corridor is illegal (see Gotchas)
- Whole-lake shallow weed flats — reservoir is "a large expanse of shallow, open marsh habitat"; mid-summer shore fishing is difficult due to dense aquatic vegetation, favouring boat anglers working weed edges — https://www.conservationhalton.ca/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/bronte_creek_watershed_study_-_a_-_final_-_2002.pdf ; https://www.anglersatlas.com/place/119719/mountsberg-reservoir
- Reputation claim: "angler's mecca for largemouth bass with fish hitting the scales at more than 5 lbs"; spinnerbaits and surface baits recommended over the weed; bass activity concentrated early morning / late evening — (source: tourism listing, https://tourismburlington.ca/what-to-do/sports-recreation/fishing/ via search snippet)

### Northern Pike
- Shallow marsh bays and weed edges throughout — the reservoir "supports a vibrant warmwater fish community" and pike are abundant enough that the watershed study calls for a "northern pike management strategy" — https://www.conservationhalton.ca/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/bronte_creek_watershed_study_-_a_-_final_-_2002.pdf
- Pike are the most-logged species on Angler's Atlas (11 catches) — open-water weed edges and pockets navigable by canoe/kayak; thick weed limits trolling motors in places — https://www.anglersatlas.com/place/119719/mountsberg-reservoir ; (source: angler report — user-contributed, search snippet)

## Notable facts (defensible claims)
- The reservoir's marsh has been designated a provincially significant waterfowl staging area and a regionally significant waterfowl breeding area, and "supports a vibrant warmwater fish community" — Bronte Creek Watershed Study (Conservation Halton, 2002) — https://www.conservationhalton.ca/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/bronte_creek_watershed_study_-_a_-_final_-_2002.pdf
- Mountsberg Dam (1967) is one of Conservation Halton's four major flood-control dams built in the aftermath of Hurricane Hazel (1954) — https://www.conservationhalton.ca/natural-hazards/
- Northern pike are prominent enough that the watershed study recommended a pike management strategy to limit their impact on the native fish community — https://www.conservationhalton.ca/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/bronte_creek_watershed_study_-_a_-_final_-_2002.pdf
- Local tourism promotion calls Mountsberg "an angler's mecca for largemouth bass" with fish over 5 lb — https://tourismburlington.ca/what-to-do/sports-recreation/fishing/ (promotional source — corroborated by CH species list and angler logs, but treat the 5 lb figure as promotional)
- The reservoir is one of only a handful of Conservation Halton properties where recreational fishing is permitted (Kelso, Hilton Falls, Mountsberg, Robert Edmondson) — https://www.conservationhalton.ca/activities/fishing/
