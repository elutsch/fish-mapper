# Lake Primer

## Physical character
Flood-control reservoir on the Conestogo River (a Grand River tributary) behind the Conestogo Dam, northwest of Elmira/Waterloo. Y-shaped impoundment following the former river valley, with two arms extending up the drowned channels.

## Morphology signal
Mid-sized reservoir, mixed depth — shallower stained water up the river arms, deeper water toward the dam. Reservoir levels fluctuate seasonally, with drawdowns tied to dam operations and rehabilitation work.

## Watershed & access
Part of the Grand River watershed, managed as a Grand River Conservation Authority reservoir. Public access through the Conestogo Lake Conservation Area, with a concrete boat launch; power boating permitted.

## Managing authorities
- Grand River Conservation Authority (GRCA)
- Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources (FMZ 16)

## Fishery reputation signal
Reservoir known for walleye, northern pike, and smallmouth bass, with black crappie and sunfish in the weedier arms. Brown trout, pike, and walleye also reported in the river below the dam.
