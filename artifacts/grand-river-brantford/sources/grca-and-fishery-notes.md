# Cached source notes — Grand River at Brantford fishery

## GRCA — Fishing Rivers and Streams
URL: https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/fishing/fishing-rivers-and-streams/ — accessed 2026-07-20
- Paris-to-Brantford = "Exceptional Waters Reach ... in part because of the high quality of the fishing." Special Ontario regs apply.
- Reach species: smallmouth bass, rainbow/brown trout, walleye, channel catfish, black crappie, carp, redhorse suckers, panfish.
- Suits wading, canoe, drift boat. Access: downtown Paris municipal parks, Brant Conservation Area, several Brantford municipal parks.
- Brantford-to-Caledonia: slow-moving, wide variety of habitats; smallmouth, walleye, rainbow trout, black/white crappie, mooneye, freshwater drum, northern pike, carp, panfish; access at bridges/parks; note most riverbank land privately owned.

## GRCA — Exceptional Waters (Grand River, Paris to Brantford)
URL: https://www.grandriver.ca/our-watershed/fisheries-management/exceptional-waters — accessed 2026-07-20
- Season extended; barbless hook, artificial lure, catch-and-release zone.
- Habitat supports smallmouth bass, walleye, northern pike, rainbow trout; species at risk: black redhorse, river redhorse.
- Cold water enters from Whitemans Creek and springs; river lined by "virtually untouched Carolinian forest."

## GRCA — Brant Conservation Area
URL: https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/conservation-areas/brant/ — accessed 2026-07-20
- 119 Jennings Road, Brantford (approx 43.14346, -80.31137). In a bend of the Grand near Six Nations and Mississaugas territories.
- Canoe/kayak entry points above and below Wilkes Dam; marked portage around Wilkes Dam; separate launch off entry road accesses river below dam.
- Powerboats and jet skis prohibited (shallow water).
- Fish from shore or canoe for pike, pickerel (walleye) and bass; below Wilkes Dam popular. Whitemans Creek nearby = fly fishing for rainbow/brown trout.
- Season May 1 – Oct 15.

## Discover Brantford / Wilkes Dam
- Wilkes Dam: 1850s low-head dam (George S. Wilkes) diverting water to a canal for downstream mills. SC Johnson Trail leads to Wilkes Dam viewing platform. River access above and below the dam; marked portage.
- Downstream of Wilkes Dam popular for pike, pickerel (walleye), bass from shore or canoe/waders.

## Angler's Atlas — Grand River, Zone 16
URL: https://www.anglersatlas.com/place/723165/grand-river — accessed 2026-07-20 (source: angler report — user-contributed)
- Most-logged: Smallmouth Bass (24), Northern Pike (22), Walleye (13), Largemouth Bass (11), Channel Catfish (9), Yellow Bullhead (3).
- Around Brantford: single barbless hooks, no live bait.

## thefishinguide.com — Grand River
URL: https://www.thefishinguide.com/grand-river.php — accessed 2026-07-20
- Abundant species: walleye, small/largemouth bass, perch, crappie, pike, carp, brown trout, steelhead, channel catfish, white bass.
- Areas downstream of the Brantford dam and Dunnville dam = "renowned channel cat fisheries"; best cat season early spring; still-fish cut suckers.
- River smallmouth "regularly become air born once hooked."

## Drift Outfitters — Lower Grand
URL: https://driftoutfitters.com/blogs/river-resources/grand-river-lower-section — accessed 2026-07-20
- Walleye season opens May, fishing through November bass closure.
- Below Brantford and Dunnville dams = renowned channel cat fisheries.

## Canadian Heritage Rivers System — Grand River
URL: https://chrs.ca/en/rivers/grand-river — accessed 2026-07-20
- Grand River designated a Canadian Heritage River in 1994; longest river entirely within southwestern Ontario; 82+ fish species.

## Bridge / boundary geography
- Reach point (data/spots.json): 43.1391, -80.2707 — between Wilkes Dam and downtown Brantford, upstream (north/west) of the downtown Lorne Bridge (~43.137, -80.268) and the Brant's Crossing pedestrian bridge. Places the reach WITHIN the Paris-to-Brantford Exceptional Waters catch-and-release section (which ends at the downtown pedestrian and service bridge).
- Brant's Crossing: steel pedestrian/cyclist bridge over the Grand between Lorne Bridge and the TH&B Crossing bridge; former 1875/1913 railway bridge converted to pedestrian use in 1996 (spans removed for replacement Fall 2025).
