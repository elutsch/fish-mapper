# Lake Primer

## Physical character
Small dammed reservoir (historic mill pond) on Black Creek in Acton, Halton Hills. Created in the 1830s by a dam that still maintains water levels; the site was a wetland before impoundment. Not to be confused with the Fairy Lakes at Huntsville or Newmarket.

## Morphology signal
Small, shallow, warm-water urban pond bordered by Prospect Park and residential shoreline. Wetland-origin basin — weedy, warm-water character suited to largemouth, pike, and panfish; no cold- or deep-water habitat expected.

## Watershed & access
Headwaters of Black Creek in the Credit River watershed. Municipally surrounded parkland (Prospect Park / Rotary Park) with easy shore and paddle-craft access; hosts a community fishing derby.

## Managing authorities
- Town of Halton Hills (owns/manages the lake and dam, Prospect Park)
- Credit Valley Conservation
- Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources (FMZ 16)

## Fishery reputation signal
Local community fishery known for largemouth bass, northern pike, black crappie, and pumpkinseed. Shore- and paddle-based urban pond fishing rather than a destination lake.
