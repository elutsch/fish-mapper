# Lake Primer

## Physical character
Flood-control reservoir on the North Thames River behind the Fanshawe Dam, on the northeast edge of London. Built in the early 1950s by the conservation authority primarily to reduce downstream flooding through the city.

## Morphology signal
Mid-sized reservoir following the former river valley — mostly shallow with deeper water toward the dam. Water levels are fairly stable through the open-water season but can rise after heavy rain; river-fed water tends turbid/stained.

## Watershed & access
Part of the Upper Thames River watershed, managed by the Upper Thames River Conservation Authority within Fanshawe Conservation Area. Fishing from shore or by boat, with launch access through the conservation area (seasonal, fee-based park access).

## Managing authorities
- Upper Thames River Conservation Authority (Fanshawe Conservation Area)
- Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources (FMZ 16)

## Fishery reputation signal
Reservoir known for smallmouth and largemouth bass, northern pike, and yellow perch, with a smaller walleye population and panfish such as pumpkinseed.
