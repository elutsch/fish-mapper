# Lake Primer

## Physical character
A flowing reach of the Grand River as it runs through Brantford, part of the Paris-to-Brantford stretch of the Canadian Heritage-designated Grand River. Low-head Wilkes Dam sits on the reach; the river alternates riffles, runs, and pools between rock/gravel bars and forested banks.

## Morphology signal
River reach, not a basin — no surface-area or depth figures apply. Shallow, wadeable in stretches with deeper pools and slack water below Wilkes Dam; moderate current over rock and gravel; generally clear to lightly stained flowing water. Too shallow for powerboats through much of the reach.

## Watershed & access
Part of the Grand River watershed, managed by the Grand River Conservation Authority; FMZ 16 regulations set by Ontario MNR. Public access via Brant Conservation Area and Brantford municipal parks; carry-in canoe/kayak with a marked portage around Wilkes Dam. Six Nations of the Grand River and Mississaugas of the Credit territories lie along this stretch.

## Fishery reputation signal
River reach known for smallmouth bass (the Paris-to-Brantford stretch carries a special catch-and-release "Exceptional Waters" reputation), with walleye and northern pike also targeted; channel catfish are noted below the Brantford dam. Panfish and crappie present in slower water.
