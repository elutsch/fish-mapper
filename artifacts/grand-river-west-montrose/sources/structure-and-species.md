# Cached: structure & species notes (guide/CA sources)

## GRCA — Fishing Rivers and Streams
Source: https://www.grandriver.ca/outdoor-recreation/fishing/fishing-rivers-and-streams/ — accessed 2026-07-20
- Grand River Shand Dam to West Montrose (tailwater): world-renowned brown trout; special regulations; species include brown trout, smallmouth bass, northern pike, walleye, carp, white sucker, bullhead, yellow perch.
- Grand River West Montrose to Bridgeport: transition/warmwater; best fished from canoe or small boat, with good shorebound/wading areas; species smallmouth bass, northern pike, carp, panfish; several trails and public parks provide access.

## Ontario Trout & Steelhead guide
Source: https://ontariotroutandsteelhead.com/fishing-the-grand-river-ontario/ — accessed 2026-07-20 — (guide blog; lower trust)
- West Montrose reach: wide, often over 70 feet, runs shallow with a mix of faster rapids/riffles and slow "frog water."
- As the river warms toward West Montrose, brown trout give way to mainly smallmouth bass; from here to Lake Erie smallmouth are found.
- Warmwater species: carp, bass, pike, walleye, panfish. Smallmouth hold in rock eddies and deeper pools that fast water flows into; forage crayfish and insects. All methods work; miles of accessible water.

## Ontario Fisherman — Grand River regs section guide
Source: https://ontariofisherman.ca/grand-river-fishing-regulations/ — accessed 2026-07-20 — (blog; regs not authoritative — used ontario.ca for regs)
- Special trout regs extend Shand Dam through Fergus/Elora to ~West Montrose / Pilkington 8th Line; below that the river is warmwater following zone defaults, no special restrictions.

## Township of Woolwich — new river access point
Source: https://www.woolwich.ca/news/posts/grand-opening-of-a-new-river-access-point-in-west-montrose/ — accessed 2026-07-20
- Access point #204 (km from Grand's mouth at Port Maitland). West Montrose Family Campground, 6344 Line 86, West Montrose, ON N0B 2V0. Carry-in for tubers/kayakers/canoeists. Open mid-May to mid-October; ample parking; sign in at front office. Managed via Township of Woolwich, West Montrose Family Camp, RTO4. Self-guided float from Steel Bridge (Township Rd. 60) to campground.

## Holmstrom Ruddick — canoe account West Montrose to Conestogo
Source: https://holmstromruddick.com/the-grand-river-of-southern-ontario-8-west-montrose-to-conestogo/ — accessed 2026-07-20 — (blog; lower trust)
- Highway 86 bridge crosses upstream of the covered bridge (before it, traveling downstream). Swampy backwaters in this section. Cox's Creek enters from the east. Conestogo River joins the Grand ~1.5 km below Conestogo village.

## Wikipedia — West Montrose Covered Bridge
Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Montrose_Covered_Bridge — accessed 2026-07-20
- Built 1880–1881 by John (and Benjamin) Bear; ~205 ft / 62 m, two spans; oak and white pine. Only remaining historic covered bridge in Ontario. "Kissing Bridge." Crosses the Grand River in Woolwich Township at ~43.5855, -80.4814. Heritage designations 1960 / 2007.
