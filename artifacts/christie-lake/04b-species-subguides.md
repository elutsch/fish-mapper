# Species Sub-Guides: Christie Lake

## Largemouth Bass
**Parent slug:** largemouth-bass
**Tier:** Strong

### Lede
The west-end marsh is largemouth headquarters. Where Spencer Creek trickles in through the Christie Wildlife Area, the lake goes shallow, weedy, and green — a bucketmouth nursery of pads, wood, and stalled current. From there the fish spread to the causeway shallows and the Marina Pavilion docks, but the creek arm is the anchor: the warmest, weediest water on a warm, weedy reservoir, and the first place to start a search.

### How it fishes
Christie doesn't give you a spring bite — the park is closed April 1 to the fourth Saturday in June, which locks out the pre-spawn and spawn entirely, so you start post-spawn. From the late-June opener, fish shallow cover early and late: the pads and stumps of the Spencer Creek inflow, the rip-rap and shallows along the causeway, the shade under the Marina Pavilion docks. Bluegill and sunfish are the main forage, so match the panfish. It's a shallow, warm basin with no documented deep cold layer, so summer doesn't push bass far — they stay on cover, sliding to the deeper weed edges only as fall pulls the temperature down. There's no winter option here: HCA is clear that Christie never sets safe ice.

### Structure
- **Spencer Creek inflow marsh / Christie Wildlife Area (west end)**: The documented shallow, marshy creek-fed zone that becomes a shallow creek upstream — classic shallow vegetated largemouth habitat, and the warmest, weediest water on the reservoir. (Named/mapped feature; holding value is a general habitat pattern, not species-documented.)
- **Causeway (mid-park)**: A named shoreline/crossing structure shown on the park map; the causeway rip-rap and adjacent shallows are typical bass shore targets.
- **Marina / boat-launch bay (east end, by the Marina Pavilion)**: The documented access bay with docks — dock structure is typical largemouth cover, and it's also where you launch (electric only).

### Rules for this species
- Season: fourth Saturday in June to November 30 (2026: Jun 27–Nov 30) — Limit: S-6 / C-2 (combined with Smallmouth Bass) — Size: no size limit — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2026-01-01)
- Property closure: the Hamilton Conservation Authority closes Christie to fishing April 1 to the fourth Saturday in June (HCA calls it a "fish sanctuary") — an on-site rule enforced by the gated park, not a provincial regulation — https://conservationhamilton.ca/activities/fishing/
- Access: electric trolling motors only, gasoline motors prohibited — https://conservationhamilton.ca/conservation-areas/christie-lake/
- Bait: FMZ 16 is within the Southern Bait Management Zone — live or dead baitfish and leeches may not be transported into or out of the zone — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2026-01-01)

## Northern Pike
**Parent slug:** northern-pike
**Tier:** Strong

### Lede
Christie has exactly one piece of deep structure, and it's the dam. The 180-metre wall at the east end backs up the reservoir's deepest water — the summer refuge when heat flattens the shallow bite. The rest of the year, pike live up the other end, jammed into the vegetated Spencer Creek inflow where the Christie Wildlife Area feeds green water and cover into the lake. Two ends of the reservoir, one fish working both.

### How it fishes
Pike want two things here: weeds to hunt, and somewhere deeper to duck when it's hot. Spring spawning happens in the flooded west-end vegetation, but the park closure runs through late June, so you never fish that window — you meet them already set up on the weed edges. Through summer, the Spencer Creek inflow and the Christie Wildlife Area margins hold fish ambushing perch, shiners, and stray panfish along the green line. When midsummer heat thins the shallow action, the dam basin at the east end is the one deeper card to play. Come fall the shallow bite firms back up. The winter season is open on paper, but forget it — HCA is blunt that Christie never sets safe ice, so there's no on-site ice fishery.

### Structure
- **Spencer Creek inflow / west-end marsh**: The documented vegetated inflow zone that fits pike spawning and ambush habitat; pike presence itself is HCA-documented, and this is where they hunt the weed line spring through fall. (Named/mapped feature; holding value is a general habitat pattern.)
- **Christie Dam basin (east side, 180 m dam)**: The documented deepest-water feature of the flood-control reservoir sits at the dam — the general reservoir pattern makes it the place to look when summer heat pushes fish off the shallow flats.

### Rules for this species
- Season: January 1 to March 31 and second Saturday in May to December 31 (2026: May 9–Dec 31) — Limit: S-6 / C-2 — Size: no size limit — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2026-01-01)
- Property closure: the Hamilton Conservation Authority closes Christie to fishing April 1 to the fourth Saturday in June (HCA "fish sanctuary") — an on-site park rule, not a provincial regulation; it removes the early-spring pike window on-site — https://conservationhamilton.ca/activities/fishing/
- No ice fishery: HCA states Christie never has safe ice conditions — the January–March open season cannot be fished on-site — https://conservationhamilton.ca/activities/fishing/
- Bait: FMZ 16 is within the Southern Bait Management Zone — live or dead baitfish and leeches may not be transported into or out of the zone — https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16 (effective 2026-01-01)
