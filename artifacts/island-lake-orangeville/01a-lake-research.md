## Notable facts (defensible claims)
- Man-made reservoir created in 1967 by two dams flooding a cedar swamp, deciduous thicket and a small kettle lake at the headwaters of the Credit River, to augment river low flows; conservation area opened to the public in 1970. — https://www.orangeville.ca/en/visit/island-lake.aspx ; https://hikingthegta.com/2016/02/24/island-lake/
- A shallow reservoir — much of the lake is about 6 feet deep, with one deeper hole reaching ~20–22 feet in the eastern arm over the original kettle lake. — https://hikingthegta.com/2016/02/24/island-lake/ ; https://adventurefishing.ca/shop/fishing-map/southwestern-and-southern-ontario-fishing-maps/island-lake-southwestern-southern-ontario/
- The east end holds extensive submerged and standing timber — fallen trees, logs and stumps — providing cover for all species, alongside heavy weed growth in the shallows. — https://adventurefishing.ca/shop/fishing-map/southwestern-and-southern-ontario-fishing-maps/island-lake-southwestern-southern-ontario/
- Northern Pike is the most angler-observed species on the reservoir (15 confirmations on Anglers Atlas, ahead of Largemouth Bass 11 and Yellow Perch 10). — https://www.anglersatlas.com/place/124687/orangeville-reservoir (angler-contributed dataset)
- Hosts the annual Island Lake Ice Derby (2026: Feb 7–16) with cash-prize categories for Northern Pike (winning fish 825 mm), Black Crappie and Yellow Perch, plus a youth Catfish category. — https://cvcfoundation.ca/island-lake-ice-derby
- No provincial stocking record exists for the reservoir; the pike, largemouth and panfish fishery is self-sustaining/naturalized. — https://www.lioapplications.lrc.gov.on.ca/services/FishONLine/StockingList.aspx?json=true&fmz=16&stock=AllSpecies&diagnostics=false&lang=en

## Gotchas & disambiguation

This record is specifically the CVC Island Lake / Orangeville Reservoir at the Credit River headwaters; also called "Orangeville Reservoir." — https://www.anglersatlas.com/place/124687/orangeville-reservoir
